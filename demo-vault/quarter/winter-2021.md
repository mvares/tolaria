# Winter 2021

## 🔨 Projects

---
## 🔍 Review

---

### 🌀 Refactoring

I launched the new [landing page](http://refactoring.club) for Refactoring, and it was a home-run success! The newsletter closed the quarter with >2000 subscribers and €800 earned from sponsors.

Goals were nailed almost perfectly, with the exception that I couldn't launch the paid version, which remains the major goal for next quarter.

Overall, Refactoring is now a staple of my professional life. Everyone at Translated knows I write it and almost everyone I work with is subscribed to it.

My network has vastly improved, and I have every week at least a call because of it.

I have a strong feeling this can become a business and make me financially independent from a daily job within the end of the year.

### 🎡 Translated

This area has been incredibly successful 🎉 this quarter. We delivered AMP 2 in time and Marco _lifted_ my responsibilities to managing several other products: Matecat, Matesub, Matedub, Tech Support and Localization Analytics.

I am happy about this journey, but it is now also heavier and more stressful. The stress is mostly about the amount of work, while I have no complaints about anything else. I have a good relationship with everyone in my teams, which now include 15+ people, and products are interesting.

Priorities are now 1) negotiating a sizable salary increase, 2) create a routine that is sustainable for me, and 3) create a good dev process for all products.

### 📈 Finance

Goals here were literally smashed, as my net worth increased +63% from €57K to €93. This is mostly thanks to Crypto, that brought 90% of that increase.

From now on I believe I will be able to dedicate less and less time to trading, and I will have to switch to mid-long term positions.

Refactoring is also going to play a bigger and bigger part of this from the moment I launch paid subs.

### 🏃‍♂️ Personal & Process

I invested heavily in my personal setup, buying: a new chair, a webcam, and a light. I spent ~€700 but I am really happy of the result! I feel more confident and motivated.

The Oculus Quest has been a smashing success: I play table tennis every day for at least 30 mins, I feel like I am really good at it, and it serves both as physical exercise and my personal fun place.

I have started playing guitar again! All thanks to the wireless jack that dad bought me for Xmas, which "freed" me from cables and all complexity. I play for a few minutes but almost every day. Really happy about it.

I am working really hard and I feel I am a bit tired. Both Refactoring and Translated are doing well but it's unsustainable in the long run. I updated the ‣ with some goals I should reach to leave Translated.

I have also been playing chess from time to time!
