# Summer 2021

---

## 🔍 Review

---

We did it! 🎉 

This was THE quarter, where everything that had been prepared in the past 9 months had to come to the pivotal conclusion of resigning from Translated to work full-time at Refactoring.

And it did!

### 🎡 Translated

Stress increased because of the enormous amount of projects I had to manage. At the same time, I felt my impact on the projects itself diminished, together with my motivation to work on them.

However, there were a few highlights:

- 🐈 Matecat team adopted Clubhouse and started working on the new Glossary, as by the roadmap I had set with Alessandro. The survey also proved useful to align everyone on priorities.
- 🖥️ AMP became adopted by ~70% of AMs, with the remaining being blocked mostly by old habits.
- ✍️ Blog picked up some steam because of the work by Kirti, who had been hired only for the purpose of writing articles about MMT.
- 🏅 Tech Support was picked up by Marta Hontanon, much to her (and my own) happiness.
A few things didn't go well instead:

- 🎙️ Matedub stayed a hot mess.
- 🗯️ Matesub didn't find any paying customer.
Right after summer I told Marco Trombetti I would resign to work full-time on Refactoring. He was very supportive (proposed to invest in Refactoring too — I declined for now) and proposed to continue working together in some fashion.

We settled with working 8-10 hours / week on a new project, called CMP, for €2500 / month. This was perfectly in line with the goal to Setup a meaningful side gig.

I am not very happy about my contribution to Translated, but I feel I got along well with people and had some success (AMP, Matecat, Tech Support).

Meanwhile, Disheng Qiu is doing great and has been promoted to Director of Engineering 😊 really happy for him and he deserves the role 100%. 

### 🌀 Refactoring & 🧠 PKM

Refactoring grew to almost 12K subscribers, and I invested heavily on Twitter as my primary network for growth. 

I started to create 🌱 evergreen notes out of articles, and to tweet them periodically via Hypefury.

My PKM improved beyond all expectations — because it has a purpose now. I am very happy about it.

Workload also became heavier though, with more people reaching out for advice, and more calls and events in general. A few of the things happened in the past 3 months:

- I became GM of No-Code at Untitled, which required a lot of work but also prepared me for the community work I will have to perform for Refactoring.
- Joined Untitled conference as a speaker.
- Did a speech at the Technical Debt Meetup by Polina Efimova
- Been working as a "content advisor" with Alex Circei and the Waydev guys, for about $1500 / month.
- Had an awful lot of calls with refactoring readers
### 👩🏻‍⚕️ Flavia

Relationship with Flavia got a bit tense in the past few months, for several reasons.

- I was more stressed because of more Translated + Refactoring work
- She was more stressed because of hard work shifts.
Tension mounted and it got to a peak when she had kind of a flirt with Mario, that stayed platonic, but she admitted she went too far.

We talked frankly and now we are better, the best we have been in many months. 

### 🏡 Family

Family health took a downturn with Andrea entering severe depression as a combination of 1) disappointing PhD work and 2) COVID stay-at-home.

He agreed to see a psychotherapist (got a contact from Matteo Colò) so let's see what happens. Mom and dad also got dragged down by Andrea.

Summer vacation in Tuscany didn't go very well too, with Andrea being very anxious, dad having a car accident, and in general we all finding little peace during that time.

I hope I will be able to spend more time with Andrea, now that I am going to work at Refactoring.

### 💪 Health

Nothing new under the sun! I believe this quarter I will get back at regular training, combining strength training with a real sport — either Tennis or Swimming

### 🏦 Finance

Crypto grew a bit, while stocks didn't. Need a better plan to manage both.
