# Autumn 2021

## 🔨 Projects

---

## 🔍 Review

---

This quarter was beautiful and hard.

### 🎒 Lifestyle

The transition from Translated to Refactoring was not straightforward. There was a bit of a slump initially where I could not be productive all day, and I felt I worked way less than I could (and should). While my goals were certainly self-imposed and not mandatory, it still feels bad when you fall short of your own expectations. 

Things got better over the quarter anyway: I found my rhythm and my productivity now stands between 10 and 12 pomodoros per day, which is fine if you ask me.

### 🌀 Refactoring

We matched all Refactoring goals exactly right, but it was not easy. 

The community launch and subsequent maintenance took an awful lot of time — and it still does. The community work is now comparable to the writing, but it is also the most rewarding area of Refactoring. I love what I am building and I am experimenting new things every day.

I also successfully launched the 🎁 Deals Program and the 🏛️ Library, both leading to a decent amount of subscriptions. 

It turns out 200 paid subscribers was the right number for the EOY (closed with ~220).

More importantly, the influx of paying subscribers doesn’t seem to slow down, which makes me hopeful about next year.

### 👩🏻‍⚕️ Flavia

This was the hardest quarter ever for my relationship with Flavia. Her stress for the end of the specialization and loss of interest towards our relationship led to a heartbreaking “pause” we took over the Christmas holidays. We stayed apart for almost 2 weeks, with little to no contact. I even spent 5 days in an Airbnb in Rome (very nice penthouse by the way), not telling it to my family, while she was alone at our place.

At the time of writing, she said the pause was a mistake and she wants to work on our issues, possibly including couples therapy.

So there it seems to be a happy ending, even if some issues remain, like her potential desire to move to southern Italy.

All in all, the pause was beneficial to me to regroup, understand that a future without Flavia is possible anyway, and get a better perspective of my life and needs.

I want to work hard to make our relationship work, but I also want to keep healthy boundaries that keep my needs in consideration.

### 💼 Consulting

The consulting gig for Translated went better than expected, as I was able to negotiate a ton of money — €2500/month for 1 day/week of work — and then work even less than agreed. 

The deal with Waydev came to a halt, instead, as they disappeared completely ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

New good opportunities appeared too:

- I may start mentoring / coaching other managers via BestPracticer, at the incredible rate of $200 / hour.
- I will likely close a deal with CTO Academy to do some lectures there, and maybe rotate Refactoring content to their newsletter, in exchange of them buying licenses for students.
### 💪 Health / Workout

I successfully reprised training based on the old program, and I kept it more or less for the whole quarter. 

However, I felt I started losing interest in December, and I will probably need to adjust this quarter. Accountability + external motivation could be the key.

I also studied more nutrition and metabolism. I would love to try Levels or Veri. 

I totally failed to 🏓 Play a Sport Regularly instead.

### 🔮 Crypto / Web3

I spent serious time studying the web3 tech, and this week I will publish the first of a two-part article about it. 

I love this space and would like to build something with it. I also found some friends who are interested as well, at least for the investing opportunity. Constantin Lipcaneanu and Armando de Lillis.

This quarter (and year) proved that even if BTC stays still, other projects may grow big time.

### 🏦 Finance

Besides the web3 space, I studied a lot about investing mostly thanks to Armando de Lillis and the Ben Felix channel. 

I feel more grounded and balanced in my investing view. I am happy with the result so far, even if in terms of gains last quarter was pretty much flat.

### 🏡 Family

Family situation kind of improved, especially over December as I spent more time with them. I feel more connected with Andrea Rossi, and he also seems to be better.

He may join Amazon for an internship (🤞) and he started helping me with the Refactoring community 👏
