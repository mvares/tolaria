## 🔍 Review

---

This quarter (Oct 2020 → Dec 2020) was truly incredible. It built on top of the foundations laid during Summer 2020, and smashed most of the goals I had set 💥

Covid is still in full force, so I have worked pretty much 100% from home — but this has allowed to develop a lot of initiatives that I wouldn't have done otherwise.

### 🌀 Refactoring

Refactoring closed the year with 800 subscribers, in the first three months. It's a result that goes beyond all expectations (goal was 200), but there's more to that than just the number of subscribers.

Refactoring has incredibly boosted my professional relationships, the perception of my role within Translated, and my self-esteem too. It has become the central point of my professional life, that sparks conversations on Linkedin, Twitter, and other communities.

Thanks to Refactoring, I have joined communities such as Lenny's, Blogging For Devs, Dev Interrupted, and CTO Craft — making new connections with great people.

I am writing one article per week, up from the bi-weekly schedule I had set originally. This has helped engagement enormously.

I have had some success with Twitter Ads, spending about €1 to acquire a subscriber.

In the last weeks, I have had some difficulties keeping the weekly pace, but I am confident I can structure my routine better to keep it sustainable.

### 🌐 Translated

Work at Translated had a slow start, but got better over time. I have been able to setup a good working process with my team, and our influence is gradually spreading across the company.

The work on AMP and the Bot is going fine, and everybody is happy with how we are working.

I have also proposed to start a Blog about Engineering and Product topics, with great reception from Marco and other team leaders. My hope is that we can build synergy with Refactoring, effectively increasing the time I can spend writing. The blog would also serve as a way of communicating more across multiple teams in the company.

Finally, a good part of this quarter's success can be ascribed to the fact that I am working from home. I have way more time at my disposal, and I don't see how I can get back to the office anymore.

### 📈 Trading

This was maybe the most unexpected highlight of the quarter. I initially set the goal of passively investing a part of my net worth, without any expectations, and ended up instead actively trading on eToro, doubling my equity, with ~$20.000 gross revenue and 13 copiers with >$300K AUM.

This success was truly sensational, and maybe the most important thing is that I really really enjoyed trading. I don't know why, but it is one of the few activities I can do effectively anytime, even when I am tired or lazy.

I have also involved Disheng and Andrea, even though they don't know the extent of my investments.

I don't know what the future will bring, but I want to keep trading.

### 🤝 Network

Refactoring kicked off a new wave of personal interactions. My network is stronger than ever, both with existing friends and new ones. I have also built a small personal CRM on Notion that reminds me of calling people in a defined days interval. 

So far it's working!

### 🏡 Family

I tried to convince Andrea Rossi to create his personal website. He set off to a promising start, but then he dropped it — I don't think he will work on it again soon. 

He also followed my steps on investing, which I am not too happy with. He is superficial, as I was too at the beginning, and I don't think he has the persistence to stick with it.

He also stopped working out (he has done Ring Fit for a while).

Mom and dad are fine, not extraordinarily well, but fine. Mom has a good relationship with Flavia, and the Christmas time we spent all together was amazing.

### 👩🏻‍⚕️ Flavia

Flavia Angelini has been stressed for a couple of months, because of the COVID situation, and because of the yearly exam — that didn't went well at the end. 

Overall, however, we spent a beautiful quarter, maybe the best ever. We consolidated our routines, spent great time together in the evenings, chatting and watching movies. 

### 🚀 Wanderio

I have stayed in touch with Matteo Colò: I thing our relationship has genuinely improved, by removing the day-to-day work interactions. I would love to have a chat in person with him, but we still can't because of COVID.

We have a call about once every two weeks.

The company will shutdown during Q1, and Matteo will stop working there at the end of Q4. He still doesn't know what he wants to do.

### 🎒 Process

I am very happy with this quarter, even though at the end I started feeling a bit tired. I pushed too hard with Refactoring, Translated and the Trading part, and I feel I need some time to step back and re-build a routine that is more sustainable.

In particular, I feel I have rushed my efforts with Refactoring in the last weeks. I have not been able to keep up with the Zettelkasten, and I will have to design something better for that too.
