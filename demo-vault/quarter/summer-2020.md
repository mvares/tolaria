# 🔍 Review

---

This quarter (July 2020 → September 2020) was arguably one of the most transformative of the recent years. I am very happy with what I have done and excited about what comes next. 

### 🚀 Wanderio

Wanderio is going to shut down, as we already knew, and will do so within the end of the year. These months have been relatively calm, as everyone got comfortable with the idea and spent some time figuring out their future.

- Matteo Colò's child, Alberto, has been born. Matteo will stay at Wanderio till the end.
- Disheng Qiu, Giorgio Pagliaro, Luca Barone and myself have accepted the Translated offer.
### 🌐 Translated

I have accepted an offer by Marco Trombetti to join Translated as Head of Engineering. This is officially my second job, the first after Wanderio. 

I am excited at the idea of turning around the engineering process here at Translated. My personal goal would be eventually to take responsibility of the overall team. 

At the time of this review, I have been working here for only two weeks, so it's hard to say anything substantial. Things move quite slow, but we see improvements.

### ✍️ Reading / Writing Habit

This is probably the biggest success of the quarter. I have set up my own Zettelkasten and written way more than I expected: about 120 Evergreen Notes , and 4 articles for my upcoming newsletter.

Everything is done on Notion, and while I always think there might be better ways to do it (e.g. Roam), so far the process is working and I am not going to change it.

### 🌀 Refactoring

At the time of this review, I am very close to launch my blog/newsletter, which fits an overall strategy of personal growth, networking, and individual creative work. I have already written 4 posts and I will probably write one more before the launch.

- EDIT: Launched it right before the end of the quarter! 🎉
This is something I am going to do for myself above all. Then, I will be happy if it creates value in other ways. 

The biggest unknown is about consistency: will I be able to write a post every two weeks? Will it be too much of an effort?

### 💪 Training

I have basically continued my regular training, even though I have felt my motivation has been decreasing over July and August. In September, I have started a new routine with 15-minute sessions every workday. Let's see how this works.

### ⚽ Fantacalcio

I have renewed my effort for Lega del Casale, by building a website on Notion — Lega del Casale — and being more active on the WhatsApp group. 

However, I discontinued Fuorigioco. It is a good idea but it doesn't fit my personal goals. This is a mistake I have done several times in the past and that I have become better at countering: deciding what to do not only based on how good something is in itself, but also based on how it fits within my bigger strategy.

Maybe, in the past it was harder because I didn't have a strategy at all, so my judgement changed more erratically.

### 🏡 Home & Family

During this quarter, with Flavia Angelini we have been talking about planning and future events A LOT. This is a marked departure from the past, and a very welcome one for me.

The good thing is we are pretty much aligned on anything, and it wasn't trivial. 

We are planning to move to a villa in the suburbs within the next 3 years, have a baby and possibly a dog or a second baby (the dog vs second baby is still under negotiation).

Flavia shares my same vision about other things as well, like remote work and have profound sea experiences (e.g. on a boat, or diving). It is beautiful to make plans about your life with your special one. I plan to do it continuously.

Overall, my relationship with Flavia got a lot better during this quarter. We spent more quality time together, we talked a lot more and it feels we now share a more profound bond. I am very happy about this.

Mom's mood got better, although she is still worried about Andrea. I am doing my best to support them, but I am starting to think that the best strategy to support mom is to focus most of my energy on Andrea, which should have a cascading effect on her.

### 🌀 Process

July and August have been "perfect" months where I managed to do almost everything I planned. With the start of work at Translated, my free time (obviously) dramatically dropped, and I have to up my process game. I need to schedule my time precisely and remove distractions. 

I think the Journal has become a bit cumbersome to manage, and needs some refactoring
