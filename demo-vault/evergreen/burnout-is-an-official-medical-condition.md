# Burnout is an official medical condition

In 2019, the [WHO declared](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/physician-health/who-adds-burnout-icd-11-what-it-means-physicians) burnout an official medical condition. It defined it as a syndrome that results from “*chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed*”.

According to the handbook, doctors can diagnose someone with burnout if they meet the following symptoms:

1. Feeling of energy depletion or exhaustion

1. Increased mental distance from their job, or feelings of negativism or cynicism related to it

1. Reduced professional efficacy

[Christina Maslach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christina_Maslach) has studied burnout for her whole career, and identified the six main areas of the work life that contribute to your stress.

In this past Refactoring article 👇 I went through the six areas and also covered 1) how you can monitor your state, and 2) how you can relieve stress with practical routines that help your mind, your body, and your relationships.
