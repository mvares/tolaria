# Keeping a steady beat — small loop vs big loop

In my recent chat with Henry Poydar, we talked about [Continuous Coordination](https://continuouscoordination.org/) — an open-source framework providing principles and practices for running modern engineering teams. 

Out of the seven key principles, one of them stuck with me: **keeping a steady beat **🥁 

This is is about adding *structure* to asynchronous coordination. Henry believes the work of most teams can be organize around two loops:

- **Small loop** — a frequent, inexpensive ceremony for team-level communication (similar to async stand-ups)

- **Big loop** — a larger, less frequent loop for updates on objectives or goals.

A successful *steady beat* avoids the "alignment trap" of either too many meetings or complete absence of synchronization.

![image](../assets/10cbdf02815c80b8.png)

While this is very high level and not prescriptive about implementation, I found it helpful to establish a *shared vocabulary* about alignment meetings.

Now I often find myself thinking: is this a small loop or big loop thing?
