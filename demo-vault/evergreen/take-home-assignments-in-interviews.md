# Take-home assignments in interviews

Tasks assigned to the candidate to be done at home. They usually require a few hours of work — more than that is unfeasible; less than that you would just do it live in an interview.

Home assignments are good for several reasons:

- They can be designed to test something that resembles a **real-world task**.

- They can be done **asynchronously** — removing **stress** from the candidate and freeing up time for your team.

- They serve as a great basis for a **follow-up interview** where you discuss the task itself.

The main drawback of such tests is that they are **high churn** — they have a high share of non-completions — and they also make your **hiring cycle longer**, as you have to give some days to the candidate to complete the test.

[Julien Evano](https://www.linkedin.com/in/julienevano/?originalSubdomain=au), Director of Engineering at IXUP, commented on this 👇

> In a high demand market, we received a lot of feedback to remove the take-home test. However, we still see a lot of value to assess a candidate on the ability to code on their own and on several occasions helped us to filter candidates early in the process.

Personally, I am a strong supporter of *take-homes*. Upsides are strong, while downsides are dubious. Candidates who churn have probably low conviction or would have failed the test anyway.
