# Hardcore vs Calm is about leverage and trust

Jean argues that a lot of this swinging is a reflection of the *leverage* engineers have.

In fact, remote work is very obviously a *wellbeing* theme. For a lot of time, companies have tried hard to make engineers happy, with flexibility being a big perk that has a real impact on people’s lives. The downturn made such a leverage shift, and the pendulum is swinging back.

So, this whole divide might boil down to the divide between two lines of thinking:

- **Happy engineers == productive engineers**, or

- **Happy engineers == lazy engineers**.

Which, in the end, is about how much leaders *trust* their people.

Executives, especially in the US, are also biased. In big hubs, like San Francisco, it is super expensive to live close to where offices are, and executives are the ones who can afford that. Also, *in-person* is how companies have been run in the past decades, so that’s what most leaders know best.

So, in the end, the recent RTO push might be more about familiarity and misaligned incentives, rather than productivity.
