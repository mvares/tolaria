# The 3x3 of what I read

When presented with this overwhelming list of stuff, I took a step back and reflected on what I truly care about. It turns out it’s three things:

- 📰 **News** — stuff that happens in tech every day, in breadth more than in depth. I don’t care about e.g. deep dives about big tech strategies, but I care that Meta or OpenAI released a new thing, or that Sam Altman said some outrageous thing in an interview.

- 🔧 **Tools** — I am a sucker for tools, and I really like to try new ones. It’s both about improving how I do thing, and about keeping my taste *fresh* about what quality software looks like. 

- ✏️ **Stories** — or call them essays, or opinions. Insights from the experience of individual people, like the ones I write on Refactoring.

This is a very basic categorization, but was already helpful to me to create a balanced *portfolio*.

Another way to *categorize* sources is by type of publication, rather than topic. I find there are three different types you can read:

- **Listings** — which provide lists of items voted by a community. E.g. [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/), [Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/), [Lobsters](https://lobste.rs/), of course Reddit, and in a way also [Techmeme](http://techmeme.com/).

- **Magazines** — which provide *many* articles every day written by a variety of authors. E.g. [The Verge](http://www.theverge.com/), [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/), [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/).

- **Individual blogs** — which provide sporadic articles (e.g. once a week, or less) always written by the same author. E.g. Refactoring, [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/), [Charity Majors](http://charity.wtf/).

### 📚 What about books?

Finally, what about books? I have a hard time reading non-fiction books regularly, which is why [we have the book club](https://refactoring.fm/p/how-i-read-books?utm_source=publication-search) — accountability, again!

Thanks to the book club, over the past two years I have read on average one book every two months, which is the *most *I have ever read in my life.

Currently we are reading [The Phoenix Project](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/17255186-the-phoenix-project), which I already read about 10 years ago, and was one of my inspirations for Refactoring!
