# Celebrating 500+ evergreen notes

A couple of years ago I read [**How to Take Smart Notes**](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/34507927-how-to-take-smart-notes)** **and it changed forever the way I think about knowledge. In particular, I loved the idea of creating a growing body of atomic, *evergreen* notes about my knowledge, from which to draw for just about anything.

So whenever I write a new article, I take its most important points and store them as individual notes on Notion:

![image](../assets/c3bd59af628c4b7b.png)

I implemented my own — very lightweight — version of the [Zettelkasten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zettelkasten) method, explained in the book.

![Example of an Evergreen Note](../assets/dc04a25f3d9f48d0.jpg)

As of today, I have amassed **500+ notes **that I use in two main ways:

-  💡 **Monday Ideas **— for writing the free Monday emails — like this one! — I just take three ideas from my notes.

- 🐦 **Social media **— all my social media posts come from individual notes.

It seems like a nerdy thing, but it’s probably been the best investment I ever made for Refactoring.
