# Async stand-ups work well

[Untitled](https://www.notion.so/3c5dfbb8060e4789a3d0f72161c41dee), which are very important, but in a remote setting they do not necessarily require an expensive daily call.

In fact, [Untitled](https://www.notion.so/f60994dfaf2c45d4aadfd4659b367d28).

You can have daily async stand-ups, and real-time stand-ups only once / twice a week when you really need them (e.g. for more alignment).

---

Do you need daily stand-ups?

Stand-ups have three major goals:

1. They make people *commit* on daily goals

1. They make sure there are *no blockers*

1. They provide a sense of *teamwork* and *belonging*

In a remote setting, these do not necessarily require an expensive daily call.

You may have async stand-ups every day, and real-time stand-ups only once / twice a week when you really need them (i.e. when stronger alignment is needed). 

We did so by using [@statushero](https://twitter.com/statushero)

This totally depends on your team, though.

Newly formed teams may need to communicate more to perform at their best.

Teams who have been working together for long, on the contrary, may get rid of the call altogether.

Consider the four (+1) stages of team development by Bruce Tuckman.

[https://hr.mit.edu/learning-topics/teams/articles/stages-development](https://t.co/wwVA2iep4Z)

As always, do not accept pre-made recipes, but listen to your team with an open mind.
