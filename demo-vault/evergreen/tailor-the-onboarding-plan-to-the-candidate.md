# Tailor the onboarding plan to the candidate

People learn in different ways. Some enjoy viewing tutorial videos, some enjoy learning from books, others learn the most by just opening the IDE and starting to code.

This doesn’t mean you should have onboarding material ready in any format — but you should ask for feedback and check-in often on how things are going.

Does this person need more pairing? Do they need to be left alone?

The best solutions are always a balance between hands-on tasks and study, and between solo and co-op time. Adjust this balance based on the candidate feedback over the first days and weeks.
