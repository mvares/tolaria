# Team-first attitude in coaching

A few months back we read [Trillion Dollar Coach](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/42118073) in the community [Book Club](https://refactoring.fm/book-club).

It is the story of legendary Silicon Valley’s coach Bill Campbell, who during his life advised the likes of Steve Jobs, Larry Page, Jeff Bezos, Sheryl Sandberg, and many others.

The first notable element from all the anecdotes and pieces of advice that Bill has given throughout his career, is that he approaches problems with a **team-first attitude**.

The book is full of evergreen advice on this — here are my three favorite items:

#### 1) **Smarts & Hearts **⚖️

Don’t hire people who spell team with an “I”. 

The principle is that people skills matter as much as technical skills. When hiring, Bill looked for smart people who 1) **worked hard**, 2) had **high integrity**, and 3) who were good at **empathy and communication**.

He wasn’t overly concerned with experience or tech chops — he hired people for potential.

#### 2) Encourage diversity 🧑‍🎤

Just like you can’t have a team full of quarterbacks, Bill believed a diverse team is achieve greater results.

Diversity applies to everything: background, walks of life, sexes, ethnicities, and more. Diverse teams deflect the risk of groupthink. To create truly world-class solutions, you can’t have an engineer-only team, or a man-only team, or an under-30 only team, and so on.

#### 3) King Arthur’s round table ⚔️

Also known as, don't make decisions by consensus.

When facing a decision, all perspectives should be heard and considered. The team should have an open debate about which idea is best, and possibly converge autonomously.

If the team can't make up their mind, then the manager has to step up, make the decision, and end the debate — because no decision is often worse than a bad decision.

Bill called this the “**King Arthur Round Table**” decision-making model. Most of the time, the knights can figure it out. When they can't, the king has to make a ruling.

Few people know that there is a common thread that runs through how Apple, Google, and Amazon built their legendary teams 🤔

All of their founders where advised by the legendary coach Bill Campbell. Bill coached Steve Jobs, Larry Page, Jeff Bezos, and many others, often at the same time!

Among Bill’s teachings, one thing was particularly unique: always put the team first. This may sound fluffy, but he made it practical in three ways:

1️⃣ 𝗦𝗠𝗔𝗥𝗧𝗦 & 𝗛𝗘𝗔𝗥𝗧𝗦

Bill believed in finding balance between technical skills and emotional intelligence. This reflected in his hiring philosophy:

↳ 🧠 𝗦𝗺𝗮𝗿𝘁 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗱𝗿𝗶𝘃𝗲𝗻 — look for individuals who work hard and show potential

↳ 🤝 𝗛𝗶𝗴𝗵 𝗶𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗴𝗿𝗶𝘁𝘆 — character matters as much as competence

↳ 💬 𝗘𝗺𝗽𝗮𝘁𝗵𝘆 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗰𝗼𝗺𝗺𝘂𝗻𝗶𝗰𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 — soft skills are non-negotiable

2️⃣ 𝗗𝗜𝗩𝗘𝗥𝗦𝗜𝗧𝗬 𝗔𝗦 𝗔 𝗦𝗧𝗥𝗔𝗧𝗘𝗚𝗬

Bill didn't just see diversity as a checkbox—he saw it as a competitive advantage. Here's why:

↳ 🧩 𝗖𝗼𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁𝗮𝗿𝘆 𝘀𝗸𝗶𝗹𝗹𝘀 — like a sports team, each role needs different strengths

↳ 🌍 𝗕𝗿𝗼𝗮𝗱 𝗽𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗽𝗲𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲𝘀 — diverse backgrounds lead to innovative solutions

↳ 🛡️ 𝗔𝗻𝘁𝗶-𝗴𝗿𝗼𝘂𝗽𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗸 — different viewpoints challenge assumptions and common wisdom

3️⃣ 𝗧𝗛𝗘 𝗞𝗜𝗡𝗚 𝗔𝗥𝗧𝗛𝗨𝗥 𝗠𝗢𝗗𝗘𝗟

Bill also had a unique take about good decision-making. He called it “the King’s Arthur round table” model:

↳ 🗣️ 𝗢𝗽𝗲𝗻 𝗱𝗲𝗯𝗮𝘁𝗲 — encourage all perspectives to be heard

↳ 🤝 𝗔𝘂𝘁𝗼𝗻𝗼𝗺𝗼𝘂𝘀 𝗰𝗼𝗻𝘃𝗲𝗿𝗴𝗲𝗻𝗰𝗲 — let the team reach consensus autonomously whenever possible

↳ 👑 𝗗𝗲𝗰𝗶𝘀𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗹𝗲𝗮𝗱𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗵𝗶𝗽 — as a leader, step in to make the call when convergence can’t be found.

These are not silver bullets. Bill’s strength was being able to adapt these principles to the unique contexts and challenges of those incredible companies.
