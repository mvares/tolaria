# Slow down your consumption

I’ve been reading *Lord of the Rings* for two months

From the beginning, I’ve read the whole thing aloud.

I tried slowing down *even more*, and discovered something. I slowed to a pace that felt almost absurd, treating each sentence as though it might be a particularly important one. I gave each one maybe triple the usual time and attention. This leisurely pace made Middle-Earth blossom before my eyes.

I also suspect that modern living, with its infinite supply of consumables, tends to push our rate-of-intake dials too high.

What’s paradoxical is that it’s precisely the seeking of that “good stuff” that normally drives me to eat so quickly, and miss most of what I’m seeking.

Clichés suffer the same issue: they are often **profound insights, consumed and passed on too rapidly** for their real meaning to register anymore.
