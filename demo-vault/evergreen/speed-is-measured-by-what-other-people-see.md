# Speed is measured by what other people see

Speed isn’t about effort; it’s about **lead time**.

Lead time is the **wall-clock time** between when someone asks for something and when they get it.

The right measure is *not* efficiency or typing speed or utilization or velocity or burn. **It’s about what the outside world experiences.**

---

Speed is all that matters — if you measure it right.

I have always believed there is no tradeoff between speed and quality in software. Shipping fast creates a tight feedback loop, which makes you improve faster and recover faster from mistakes.

All of this *enables* quality and stability.

But that’s only if you measure speed based on the work you actually ship.

Speed is not the amount of commits you make, or the amounts of PRs. It’s not even about the amount of *deployments*.

Speed ultimately means minimizing the *wall-clock time* between when someone asks for something, and when they get it.

It’s about what the outside world experiences.

If you think you are fast, ask yourself: would your customers say the same?
