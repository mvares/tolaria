# Organize notes in big batches instead of on the spot

The *organize* part is where you take all the mess that you captured and figure out what you should do with it.

I do ~80% of my organization in big batches, and very little on the spot. In particular, I have 1h 30m (three [pomodoros](https://refactoring.fm/p/how-to-manage-your-time) 🍅) blocked every Monday afternoon, during which I organize notes from the past week and plan my tasks for the next.

This *review* covers three areas, which roughly take 30 mins each:

1. ✏️ **Notes** — I organize all the items I captured during the previous week that are still “*unassigned*”. These include notes on Notion, Bear, photos I took last week, and downloaded files.

1. 💬 **Comms** — I go through email and DMs everywhere. I also reply to messages everyday, of course, but I find it useful to do a full pass once a week to make sure nothing falls through the cracks (something always does).

1. 🔨 **Work** — I review what I did the previous week and plan what to do for the next. This is the last part of the ceremony and for a good reason: notes and comms often inform what to do next.

So, what does it mean to*** ****organize* notes, in practice?

To me, it means figuring out **what I should do with them**, to answer the question: *why am I saving this?* Or should I, really? When I organize stuff, I end up trashing ~50% of the things I captured the previous week.

I found that one of the side effects of decoupling — also temporally — the capture from the organization, is that many things that *seemed* important on the spot, become very less so after just a few days, so you can safely remove them.
