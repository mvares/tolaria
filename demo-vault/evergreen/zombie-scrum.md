# Zombie Scrum

I made up this term for all those teams who take Scrum and use ~70% of it, adapting the rest to their workflow.

Some common tweaks are:

- Replace **daily scrum** with something lighter / async. We [did this ourselves](https://refactoring.fm/p/standups?s=w).

- **Release** work continuously instead of only at the end of the Sprint.

- Have fewer **retrospectives** — e.g. every month / quarter instead than every Sprint.

Sometimes the opposite happens too: people *believe* they hate Scrum and resort to inventing their own process, but basically end up reimplementing Scrum. I call this **undercover Scrum**.

[Yurii Mykytyn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/meako/), Director of Engineering at Rebbix, also commented on this:

> Many people who try to do Scrum didn't even bother to read the Scrum guide, and when they read it they find that the guide itself doesn't tell them to do things which people usually hate Scrum for.
