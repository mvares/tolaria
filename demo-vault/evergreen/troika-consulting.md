# Troika Consulting

In [Troika Consulting](https://www.liberatingstructures.com/8-troika-consulting/) one person brings a challenge and shares an overview for 1 minute. They then switch off their camera and go on mute (or turn around if they are in person) while the other 2-3 people discuss their questions, assumptions and solutions in relation to the challenge.

Here is the basic sequence of steps:

1. *Invite participants to reflect on the consulting question (the challenge and the help needed) they plan to ask when they are the “clients” (1 min)*

1. *Groups have first “client” share his or her question (1-2 min)*

1. *“Consultants” ask the client clarifying questions (1-2 min)*

1. *Client turns around with his or her back facing the consultants*

1. *Together, the consultants generate ideas, suggestions, coaching advice (4-5 min)*

1. *Client turns around and shares what was most valuable about the experience. (1-2 min)*

1. *Groups switch to next person and repeat steps.*

We’ve found that this is a great way to disrupt power dynamics — getting a senior engineer that has a habit of interrupting people to stay quiet and listen while other people come up with solutions can be transformational for people’s confidence and reveal ideas that may never have come to the surface otherwise.

This also works particularly well for neurodivergent people, as the rules of engagement and roles are really clear so therefore people that usually stay quiet are more likely to speak up.

It is also common that teams will gravitate too quickly to the ideas and solutions that are vocalised first, or to those who are better at making their suggestions sound more “legitimate” through the language they use. Doing an activity where you get people to draw their ideas, their feelings, or their problems can slow people down a bit and help give new clarity to what you might be trying to articulate to one another.
