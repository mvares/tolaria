# Remote improves wellbeing that improves productivity

How do these factors—remote vs office, balanced vs long hours—impact productivity and work output? Well, it’s complicated.

In engineering, and knowledge work in general, productivity only *loosely* correlates to how many hours you work. Productivity comes more from how you feel, how much you sleep, how clear the strategy is, and other intangibles.

So, the real **10x engineers are those who are well-rested and motivated**, vs those in a depleted state.

As managers, we strive to keep people motivated, but we should equally care for their energy and wellbeing. Jean tells me that she often encourages reports to **take time off**, especially parents. Those people have a lot of stuff going on — how are you going to do great work if you do not take care of that first?

And she has to *encourage* them, because many are not comfortable doing that when there is a lot on their plate. It’s on managers to 1) build processes that let people take time off safely, and 2) create a healthy culture where people are comfortable with it.
