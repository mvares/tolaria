# Second-order Thinking & First Principles

You can improve your decision-making with second-order thinking + first principles 🧠

Second-order thinking considers the future consequences of a decision, going beyond the immediate implications.

In engineering, this is the name of the game. Whenever you design a system, or create an abstraction, you are never only thinking at the problem here and now — you are also thinking at the impact on the larger system, on your team workload, on infrastructure, and more.

On a practical level, you can prompt second-order thinking in two ways:

1. ➡️ Logically • “and then what” — For any decision you are considering, ask yourself “and then what?” many times over. Do that 4-5 times and you will be surprised by how far you can go.

1. ⏳ Temporally • the rule of 10 — many choices look different under different timeframes. Ask yourself about the consequences in 10 hours, 10 days, 10 months, and 10 years.

The dual version of second-order thinking is first principles.

When you ask yourself “and then what”, you are moving forward. Likewise, you can ask yourself “why” several times (e.g. the five whys) to go backwards, and get to the principles backing the decision, or root causes in case of a problem.
