# When you are starting up, just hire generalists

When you are starting a company and your team is < 10 people, you should probably hire smart generalists. 

At this stage you are **figuring out what **to build and **how** to build it. You can't really anticipate what you will do, both in terms of tasks, and tech.

For this reason, surround you of **generalists** who are happy to do pretty much anything.

[Untitled](https://www.notion.so/0fb3abec33964d8895634acc203b9ceb), they don't bother about **switching tech**, throw in some no-code tools, and do whatever it takes to deliver the product.

On the other side of the spectrum, you probably don't want someone who is **heavily invested** in some specific technology, as they will try to use it no matter what, because they feel their career depends on that.
