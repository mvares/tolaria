# Pair programming is counterintuitive

The reason why pair programming gets so little adoption in most companies is because it is wildly counterintuitive.

In fact, it looks bad to all stakeholders:

- 💼 **To managers, it feels like a waste** — it's two devs working on the same code, when they could be doing two separate things.

- 🔨 **To developers, it feels uncomfortable** — social interaction is draining. And what about the sacred *state of flow*?

Companies who try it and stick with it for a few months, though, do not go back.
