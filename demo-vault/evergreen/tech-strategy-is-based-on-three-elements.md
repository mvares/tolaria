# Tech strategy is based on three elements

[Untitled](https://www.notion.so/0db21292b94d4fdf833da24aad5ac417), so you can design a good technical strategy by reflecting on three elements:

- 🏢 **Your Company **— what can engineering do to support your **company mission**? What should you invest in to create a **core asset **for your company, generating a hard-to-copy, **competitive advantage**?

- 📱 **Your Product **— how can engineering support your **product strategy** to deliver a superior experience that drives the company mission forward? What kind of products can be **enabled** by a strong technical strategy?

- 👕 **Your Team **— How can you leverage your engineering **team's strengths** to create something that benefits your company long term?

---

A good technical strategy is a real superpower for your company.

There are three main elements you should reflect on, to design a good one 👇

1. 🔮 Your Mission

How can tech support your company mission?

What should you invest in to create a core asset for your company, generating a hard-to-copy, competitive advantage?

Example: Zoom invested hard in proprietary streaming tech.

1. 📱 Your Product

How can engineering support your product strategy to deliver a superior user experience?

What kind of products can be enabled by a strong technical strategy?

Example: Uber's product lines (Freight, Eats, etc.) are all enabled by their fleet mgmt platform

1. 👕 Your Team

How can you leverage your engineering team's strengths to create something that benefits your company long term?

Example: Airbnb failed the React Native transition in part because its team was too invested in native mobile skills.
