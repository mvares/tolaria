# Keep some slack at work

There are many examples of companies explicitly carving out **unproductive** space for engineers. Like the famous 20% time at Google, or [Hack weeks at Dropbox](https://refactoring.fm/i/123257681/hack-weeks-bug-bashes), which we discussed with Andrew, two weeks ago.

Based on situations, these are either *space to do whatever*, or *space to enjoy*, or something in between. These spaces are great for people to decompress — and a lot of great ideas and projects come out of them anyway!

In general, leaving some slack at work is about **treating people like humans**, trusting that by taking care of their basic needs they will be able to do an overall better work.
