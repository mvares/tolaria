# The biggest offenders to engineering productivity are people

Consider the 2017 Pluralsight survey [about productivity](https://email.mg1.substack.com/c/eJxNkc-OhCAMxp9muI0BRNEDh73saxj-VIcsgoEyxrdf3LlsSvgSaPtLv1qNsKV8qSMVJPe14HWAinCWAIiQSS2QF-_UOPXDPAniFJXcSkN8WdYMsGsfFDmqCd5q9Cn-JQs6z-SlRG_twAXVIFdjqBOT4FZQwcDBxFbxQerqPEQLCt6QrxSB2LTvEPFuRYJ6IR7l0X89-Hc753l2R6hZh-K3F3Ytt72akLYmCHovTTll8lnSiqfO8HStcUgH5OeRk6sW_dvj9Sw1v-EiXnHKOR0pp0Pfouu7wUwzlY6vlg2DGeVD0H1jXammoLY_N5NkFarV7SfDqi2m7OPW2VDNbcZyT1BjoywQtQngFOYKBD9O_7m2bBAhtw24RaNi4yCYmJjgwyg_vrTpB8kknykjDe1Sq4rqH-4XYtCZBA). 

Among other things, people were asked to rate the things that drain the most productivity from their days.

![image](../assets/613df8b1e6b74bbd.png)

As it turns out, the biggest offenders are… **other people**! Either in the form of meetings, or waiting for them to do stuff, or more.
