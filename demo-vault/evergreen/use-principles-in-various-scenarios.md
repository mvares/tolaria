# Use principles in various scenarios

[Untitled](https://www.notion.so/7caf635fc2624ed69427beb4a1225735), they are useful in a number of circumstances:

- **Evaluate designs **— when creating something new, be it a process or a piece of software, you might use principles to understand if you are missing some crucial aspect of your work.

- **Onboarding **— you can get new members up to speed faster by using principles to instruct them about the way you work.

- **Post-mortems and **[Untitled](https://www.notion.so/497d5cc50d3b41ad953adcb43e7b3657)**s **— you can compare a failure against principles to spot what went wrong, and possibly adjust principles themselves to improve in the future.

- **Reviews **— principles guide people's behaviour, so you can use them to review behaviour as well. Principles provide useful review *lenses* because they go beyond pure performance. 

---

Many companies are reluctant to create engineering principles because they believe they are abstract and in the end they are not that useful.

This is false 👇

Engineering principles create alignment about how decisions should be made, which in turn enables autonomy.

More autonomy = more speed across the organization

Here are some situations in which you can use principles 👇

📝 Evaluate designs

When creating something new, be it a process or a piece of software, you might use principles to understand if you are missing some crucial aspects of your work.

🎒 Onboarding

You can get new members up to speed faster by using principles to instruct them about the way you work.

💀 Post-mortems and pre-mortems

You can compare a failure against principles to spot what went wrong, and possibly adjust principles themselves to improve in the future.

🔍 Reviews

Principles guide people's behavior, so you can use them to review such behavior as well.

Principles provide useful review lenses because they go beyond pure performance.
