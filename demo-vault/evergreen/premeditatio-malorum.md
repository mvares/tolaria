# Premeditatio Malorum

Ancient stoic philosophers, like Seneca and Marcus Aurelius, often conducted an exercise called ***premeditatio malorum****, *that translates to **premeditation of evils**.

They believed that by **visualizing negative things** (*negative visualization*) that could happen in their life, they could make **better plans to prevent them**.
