# Calls are more expensive and less effective than meetings

Calls are **more expensive and less effective** than meetings, for two reasons: 

- **Non-verbal communication**: people can't pick up non-verbal communication as well as they do in person, so they spend more energy to do so.

- **Distractions**: in a call, you are more likely to be distracted by all the other things you can do with your laptop — open chrome, check notifications, etc. It requires more energy to stay focused.

Calls are more expensive and less effective than meetings.

We all have know this intuitively, but did you know it is also backed by science?

There are two reasons:

🙋‍♂️ Non-verbal communication
🏃‍♂️ Distractions

🙋‍♂️ Non-verbal communication

In a call, people can't pick up body language as well as they do in person, so:

1. they spend more energy to do so

1. communication is worse anyway

This has been researched and proven:
[https://www.bbc.com/worklife/article/20200421-why-zoom-video-chats-are-so-exhausting](https://www.bbc.com/worklife/article/20200421-why-zoom-video-chats-are-so-exhausting)

🏃‍♂️ Distractions

In a call, you are more likely to be distracted by all the other things you can do with your laptop — open chrome, check email, etc.

It just requires more energy to stay focused.
