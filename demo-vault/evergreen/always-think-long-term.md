# Always think long-term

This is a very personal subject — the way you rank money against other stuff largely depends on your values and personal situation.

When it comes to myself, I always try to think long-term. I suspect this will never change — right now I think long-term for myself; when I am older I will probably do so for my children, and so on.

I do so because I believe in two fundamental things:

- 📈 **Compounding** — Incredible results are most often not the result of extraordinary *burst* performance, but rather the result of *adequate* performance sustained for an *uncommonly long* period of time. This stands true for everything: personal finance, health, relationships, you name it.

- 🐠 **Swimming against the current** — In modern society, long-term thinking seems both increasingly uncommon and increasingly hard. So, people who are able to do so consistently should have an edge.

That said, I know what you may be thinking now:

- “*Luca is saying I have to work my a** off and sacrifice work-life balance, at least early in my career*”

- “*Luca is saying I need to prioritize learning over money*”

This is what people most often associate with *long-term*.

So, actually, no.

Long-term applies to everything. By working long hours you may be sacrificing your relationships, or your health, for which you also want to think long-term.

So, in the end, money is just one piece of the puzzle. Our lives are made of many moving pieces and you want to be thinking at them holistically, and long-term, to make the decision that is best for you.
