# I highlight, take notes and append to article ideas

Taking good notes is crucial for my writing process. 

I read nearly everything from my *backlog*, which I keep on the [Readwise Reader](https://readwise.io/read) (see the [*reading online*](https://refactoring.fm/p/how-to-read-online)* *article). For each article I highlight the most relevant passages, and these are sent to my Notion automatically by [Readwise](https://readwise.io/) itself.

Readwise creates a note for each article I have read and highlighted, and such notes only contains my highlights. This is all done automatically.

![image](../assets/abfbe09f72a14610.png)

Once a week I review what I read the week before, link items to existing article ideas (not published yet), or create new ones specifically.

At any given time, I have tens* *of *open* article ideas — 29 right now. These are rough sketches, usually just a few bullet points. They include notes from myself, references to readings, community threads, and more.

Whenever I read something, I may think “*this can be useful for this article*” and link it there. I only start writing an article when I believe I have enough material to write a great piece. That means an idea can stay there for many months before I actually go and write a full article about it.

I only start writing an article when I have enough material for a great piece.

That means an idea can stay there for months before I actually go and write a full article about it.

I am not sure this is the *right* way of doing it — it is just my process 🤷‍♂️ I share it in case it is helpful to others!
