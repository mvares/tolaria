# Quality confidence matrix

[Christine](https://de.linkedin.com/in/christine-pinto) delivered a great talk about *quality* — this ever *elusive *concept in software.

A common approach to quality is that all changes are created equal, and should go through the same QA process. While this is *hard-and-fast* and has some merits, it also risks cornering teams into extremes:

- **Too much** — heavy quality control systems that make devex nightmarish and delay releases.

- **Too little** — move-fast-and-break-things cultures in which, after a while, you are only breaking things, and not moving fast anymore.

Christine proposes a more nuanced approach where the *confidence* in what you ship should be proportional to two values: **customer impact** and **technical risk** 👇

![image](../assets/226bdf02815c805f.png)

The more the stuff is up and to the right in the quadrant, the more you should only ship it with high confidence and high quality.

She also argued that improving quality* *in teams is a journey, which usually goes through three steps:

1. 🦸 **Crusader** → some engineer acts as a *hero* that spearheads quality, largely on their own + influencing leadership. 

1. 🪴 **Coach** → the hero teaches others, establishing quality principles and adoption.

1. 🌳 **System** → when critical mass is achieved, individual efforts turn into team processes.
