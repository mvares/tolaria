# Use Mermaid for Diagramming

[Mermaid](https://mermaid.js.org/) is a diagramming tool that uses Markdown to create and modify complex diagrams dynamically.

As you may have figured out by now, I am a visual person, and I have probably created hundreds of diagrams in my life. Well, diagrams are always a mess to manage at a team level: you move around those jpegs, lose track of the source, get fixated with style, and more.

Mermaid turns Markdown-ish code into diagrams.

![image](../assets/46a354a2de1f4b33.png)

Mermaid’s code-first approach fixes most of these issues. In fact, you can now version diagrams just like you do with code, and update them easily. You can also define themes globally, so you keep your focus on content, and save a ton of time.

Mermaid is almost universally loved and has an incredible number of [integrations](https://mermaid.js.org/ecosystem/integrations-community.html), my favorite being those with [Github](https://github.blog/2022-02-14-include-diagrams-markdown-files-mermaid/) and [Notion](https://lukemerrett.com/using-mermaid-flowchart-syntax-in-notion/): you paste Mermaid code, and get the diagram automatically displayed!
