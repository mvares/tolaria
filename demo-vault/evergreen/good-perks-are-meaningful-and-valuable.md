# Good perks are meaningful and valuable

Talking with tens of companies about their job posts on the Refactoring board, there is a recurring question about perks. 

Should we add them to the job post? Do they bring value? What perks should we include?

Here are my thoughts 👇

Include a perk in your post only if it is good. A good perk checks at least one of these boxes:

- **It is meaningful** — it should be relevant for your culture, reinforcing / encouraging substantially some of your values. For example, for a remote and async culture, it is meaningful to give a generous budget for a home workstation.

- **It is really valuable **— famously, [Whole Foods](https://careers.wholefoodsmarket.com/global/en/benefits#:~:text=Our%20most%20well%2Dknown%20benefit,that%20discount%20up%20to%2030%25.) gives 20-30% in-store discount to all their employees. 

Bad perks are the opposite of meaningful and valuable — they are arbitrary and cheap. 

They make candidates suspect you are using them to compensate for a position that is not interesting enough, or well-paid enough.

Bad perks are free snacks, gift cards, or paid birthdays off.

In this case, no perks is way better than bad perks. Plenty of great companies do not advertise them in job posts, and it's fine!

The focus here is on "advertise". It can be great to have paid birthdays off. You just don't need to write it in the job post.
