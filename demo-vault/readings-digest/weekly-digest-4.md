# Weekly Digest #4

Now I have all the information I need. Let me compile the digest:

---

#### 🥇 [Increasing Your Luck Surface Area](https://www.codusoperandi.com/posts/increasing-your-luck-surface-area)

#### 3 min • by Jason Roberts

Your luck is not random — it's a function of *doing* things and *telling* people about them. L = D × T. The more you do, and the more you share it with relevant communities, the more serendipity finds you. Simple formula, profound implications.

---

#### 🥈 [Invert, Always Invert](https://theengineeringmanager.substack.com/p/invert-always-invert)

#### 5 min • by James Stanier

Charlie Munger's classic mental model applied to engineering leadership. Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "how would I guarantee failure?" — then avoid those things. A powerful reframe for problem-solving.

---

#### 🥉 [How Good Engineers Write Bad Code at Big Companies](https://www.seangoedecke.com/bad-code-at-big-companies/)

#### 6 min • by Sean Goedecke

It's not that big companies hire bad engineers — it's that incentives shape behavior. When you're rewarded for *shipping* rather than *maintaining*, when ownership is diffuse, and when the codebase is already messy, even great engineers write bad code. Systemic problem, not a talent one.

---

#### 4) [Revisiting Manager READMEs](https://skamille.medium.com/revisiting-manager-readmes-b9a59167c226)

#### 4 min • by Camille Fournier

Years after her famous critique of Manager READMEs, Camille revisits the topic. Her take: they're still mostly bad, but there's a version that works — focused on *logistics* and *working style*, not personality manifestos or trust shortcuts.

---

#### 5) [Productivity Rain Dances](https://calnewport.com/productivity-rain-dances/)

#### 2 min • by Cal Newport

There are *busy* activities that feel productive but don't lead to real results. Productivity nerds like me are often guilty of focusing more on *inputs* (how I do stuff), rather than actual outputs (what results they bring). Great reminder and memorable mental model.

---

#### 🛠️ Tools

#### [Frictionless](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0G22PCVBG)

New book by Nicole Forsgren (co-author of *Accelerate*) about removing barriers that slow organizations down. Seven steps to unlock value and outpace competition. If you loved *Accelerate*, this is a must-read.

#### [Fizzy](https://www.fizzy.do/)

New kanban tool from 37signals (the Basecamp folks). Their take on task management: simple, opinionated, and focused on keeping lists *fresh* rather than accumulating "badlogs". Classic 37signals philosophy.

#### [PGlite](https://pglite.dev/)

Full Postgres running in your browser via WASM. Just 3.7MB compressed. Useful for local-first apps, demos, or anywhere you need a real database without a server. Surprisingly capable.

#### [Pylar](https://www.pylar.ai/)

Give AI agents safe access to your structured data. Create governed SQL views, build MCP tools, and deploy to any agent builder. For teams worried about letting LLMs loose on their databases.
