# Founder's guide to Community

## Community Strategy

---

### Audience vs Community

- Audience = you help people
- Community = you help people help each other
### Community Roles

- Moderators: keep content clean and organized in the community
- Facilitators: start conversations in the community and host discussions
- Event organizers: start local chapters and self-organize local or virtual events
- Ambassadors: advocate on behalf of the brand
- Content contributors: write articles, create videos, or develop other forms of content
- Committee members: join a customer advisory board to guide product direction
- Power users: achieve status by being the most active members of a platform
- Mentors: dedicate time to supporting other customers one on one or in small groups
### Community models

1. Support: Create spaces for customers to answer questions and solve problems for each other. Example: Autodesk support community
1. Product: Create spaces for customers to share product feedback and ideas with each other and with your team. Example: Atlassian feedback section
  1. Refactoring 🥉
1. Acquisition: Build programs that help you grow your pipeline and customer base. Example: Branch’s Mobile Growth community
1. Contribution: Enable members to contribute content, services, or something else of value to a platform you create. Example: Notion Template Gallery
  1. Refactoring 🥈
1. Engagement: Connect customers to each other around their common interests in order to increase customer retention. Example: Culture Amp’s Culture First
  1. Refactoring 🥇
1. Success: Enable customers to teach each other how to better use your product and be more successful in their careers. Example: Salesforce’s Trailblazer program
### Persona Questions

- What goals or aspirations do you have that you would like the community to help with?
- What pains or challenges do you have that you would like the community to help with?
- What objections might prevent you from participating in the community?
- How do you want to contribute? (e.g.: answer questions, host an event, create content, mentor, etc.) 
### Community Health

1. Activity: Are members participating regularly?
  1. MAU 
  1. DAU
  1. DAU/MAU (stickiness)
1. Value: Are members getting the benefits that they came for?
  1. NPS Survey
    1. What value do you expect to get from the community?
    1. On a scale of 1-10, to what extent do you feel you’re getting that value?
1. Belonging: Do members feel connected, safe, and included?
  1. Do you feel safe in the community?
  1. Do you feel like your voice is heard?
  1. Have you formed relationships with other members of the community?
  1. Do you feel included?
### Tactical-level Goals

Lay out all the specific things you plan to do in the upcoming month and quarter in order to improve community health and engagement and drive business impact.

Examples:

- Host 3 educational events
- Create a community member onboarding process
- Personally message 0 passive members
- Post in the community every morning
## How to Build

---

### The 7P's 🔗

1. People: Who the program is focused on
1. Purpose: Why they need this program
1. Place: Where members will gather
1. Participation: What members will do
1. Policy: Guidelines and rules that will shape the experience
1. Promotion: How members will learn about the program
1. Performance: What success looks like
### Launching your online community

You want to make sure everything is set up before people arrive, and host a pre-party so that when people arrive, the vibes are already flowing.

Your community’s pre-party is extremely important, because when anyone joins the community, they’ll observe how people are interacting with each other and they’ll replicate it.

### Founding members

1. Have a really clear need for the community
1. Embody the values and culture you want to create
1. Aren’t so busy that they won’t be able to take an active role in starting the community
They will do it because:

1. They like to be early adopters, and take pride in being a founding member
1. They see value in being connected to the other founding members
1. They’re bought into the vision of the community
### How to engage founding members

- Ask founding members to post thoughtful questions
- Message other founding members and ask them to jump in and answer
- Organize curated experiences for your founding members
- Invite more members and double them every two weeks (10 → 20 → 40 → 80 → ...)
