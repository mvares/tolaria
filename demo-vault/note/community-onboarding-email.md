# Community Onboarding / Email

Hey everyone, the time has come!

We have been beta-testing the Refactoring community for the past couple of months, and I am thrilled to open it up with you 🎉

But first, let's recap why we are here and why a community is the next logical step in Refactoring journey 👇 

## 💬 Why a Community?

The goal of Refactoring is to help people build great engineering teams. 

If you are a technical leader who wants to create a motivated, high-performing team, a founder who wants to scale their company, or an engineer who wants to up their game, this is the place for you.

For more than a year I have written a new essay every week about some engineering topic I care about. Even though these essays include sizeable research, they always present my own point of view. 

This is a blessing and a curse.

The upside is you get practical advice that is anchored to real-world experience. The downside is there is a ceiling to what I can genuinely write about, because there is a ceiling to my own experience.

The community is a natural extension to what Refactoring can provide, by allowing people to share and receive advice in a way that goes beyond what I am able to do on my own.

And it goes beyond advice, too. In the community you are able to meet brilliant peers and create valuable connections for your personal and professional life.

My vision for Refactoring is to become your engineering family — the place and the people you trust you can always reach out to for anything you need.

So what should you expect from it? 👇

### ✍️ Async Communication

I believe great conversations need the right time for reflection and the right space to express structured thoughts.

For this reason, the community focuses on written, async communication, where everyone can jump in at their own pace.

It is built on Circle, a modern tool that favours deep conversations over noisy, real time chats.

Whether you will be here every day or check in just when you have some time, the community is going to support you.

Every week you will receive an email digest with the best posts and comments written by community members, so it's easy to catch up if you missed something.

### 🔍 Quality

The community is exclusive to Refactoring Club (premium) members. 

The subscription is crucial to keep the quality of interactions high, limit spam, and to make people more committed to participating.

My promise is that this is going to be very different from your average Slack or Discord channel. Think of a place where you can go, share something you are struggling with, and have folks with tens of years of experience reply with their best advice.

Subscription revenues also allow to bring in guests and domain experts for specific topics, and organize all kinds of initiatives to make the community better.

### 🗓️ Events

Refactoring is as much about learning as it is about making friends and connections. You can expect regular events from the community, including:

- 🏢 Office Hours — a weekly call with me and others where you can jump in, discuss the latest Refactoring article, or any other topic you are struggling with.
- 📣 Community Talks — talks organized by community members around specific topics. 
- 🏅 Special Guests — interviews and AMAs with incredible external guests.

## ➡️ How to join

Club members will find a link to a survey below.

The purpose of this is to understand what your interests and goals are, how you like to help and talk with others, and what you think a great community looks like.

I take building this space very seriously. To make it an amazing place, I prioritize people who are excited to be a part of it, open to learn from others, and generous in offering their own skill and wisdom.

Also, I will personally reach out to everyone who fills the questionnaire to have a call and get to know each other 👋

If you are not a Club member, consider joining below! If you are already, just continue scrolling!
