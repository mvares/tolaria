# Car Setup Guide

## Car height

- front and back
- affects mechanical grip
- to counter understeer
  - need more grip front
  - lower front ride height (or raise rear ride height)
- to counter oversteer
  - need more grip back
  - raise front ride height (or lower rear ride height)
- you want car to be as close to the road as possible
## Aerodynamics

- tradeoff between grip and speed
- front grip vs rear grip
## Antirollbar

- to counter understeer
  - make rear arb stiffer
  - or make front arb softer
- to counter oversteer
  - make front arb stiffer
  - or make rear arb softer
## Camber

- provides a way to make tyres in full contact all the time
- negative camber = top of tire farther to the car than bottom \ /
- positive camber = top of tire close to the car than bottom / \
- check tire temperature with IMO (inside middle outside) method.
  - excessive inside = too much camber
  - excessive outside = too little camber
## Tire Pressure

- goal is to have as much tire in contact with the ground as possible
- low pressure = high grip = high temperature
- high pressure = low grip = low temperature
