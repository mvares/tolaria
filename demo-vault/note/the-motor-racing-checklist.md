# The Motor Racing Checklist

---

# Mental Models

1. Never wait • for things to happen
1. Relax hands • when braking to indulge rotation and FF
1. Find understeer • initially (?)
1. Create references • for 1) braking, and 2) getting on throttle
1. Brake parallel • to the white line. Check angle, create checkpoints.
1. Drive the car • first, not the line. Eventually drive both
1. String theory • apply steering inversely proportional to braking. Don’t release brakes without steering 
  - Trail braking = braking + steering

## 1.4 • Posture

- Don’t glue heel to the floor
- Steer = 9:3 position
- Back always in touch
- Hands are relaxed
- Breathe continuously
## 1.5 • Active vs Passive Driving

- Always try to maximize lateral load
- Passive = wait for things to happen
  - Usually it’s understeer
  - You should never wait
- Active driver = make things happen
## 1.6 • How to Learn a New Track

- Find lines under the limit
- Find understeer
  - Brake too much and too early, accelerate early
  - Understeer consistently
  - It gives you a workable speed range
  - You find the limit of the front tires
- Minimize understeer
  - Brake a bit later
  - Until the car is a bit oversteer-y into the corner
- Perfect lines according to each car
- Three types of corners
  - Flat
  - Close to flat
    - Just a lift or <20% brake
    - The deeper the lift the more dangerous it is (more oversteer)
  - Lot of decel required
## 1.8 • Software Settings

- Do not clip FF
- Auto on iRacing
- [ ] Check F bar in game
- Load cell pedal linear
## 1.9 • Stages of a Corner

- Hard Braking phase
- Early Entry
  - Heavy trail braking
    - Start release brakes
  - Weight transfer backwards and outwards
  - Slowly apply steering
- Late Entry
  - Still braking, max rotation point
- Early Exit
  - Still low speed
  - Safer to rotate
- Late Exit
  - Understeer snap oversteer
  - No more rotation
# 2 • Braking

## 2.1 Speed Control

- Workable speed range
- Hard braking always on a straight line
## 2.2 How to Master Racing Checklist

- [ ] Make sure you are capable of braking 1%
- [ ] Increase braking slooowly to 99%
- [ ] Decrease sloowly to 90-80%
- And do it all over again
  - To improve muscle sensitivity
- [ ] Other exercise: apply brakes fast to 99% and release slowly
## 2.3 • The Stages of Braking

- Medium / Fast application
  - How fast depends on the car, whether ABS, etc.
- Threshold braking
  - Keeping the braking pressure without locking the car or hit ABS
- Trail braking
## 2.4 Aerodynamic vs Mechanical Grip

- Mechanical Grip
  - Depends on the surface
  - Stays pretty much consistent unless elevation
- Aerodynamic Grip
  - Depends on air pressuring the car at speed
## 2.5 • Braking vs elevation changes

- Crest (more downhill) = less grip, harder to brake and accelerate
- Compression (more uphill) = higher grip, easier to brake and accelerate
## 2.6 • Brake Bias

- Distribution of braking force between front and rear brake
- Less brake bias = more rotation
- More brake bias = less rotation
## 2.7 • Dynamic Brake Bias

- Brake bias + engine braking
- Engine braking = braking when RPM higher than X based on gear
  - The higher the pitch of the engine sound, the more the engine braking
- [ ] Try to lock up fronts or rears based on gear shifting
## 2.8 • Tyre Temperature

### Short term -> Same corner

- When you brake you add temperature
  - Higher temperature typically is less grip
- When you lock up temperature spikes
  - So don’t abuse tyres with braking
- Tyres cool down on straight
### Mid-term -> Same sector

- You may carry high temperature to next corner and have less grip
### Long-term -> Whole race

- If you downshift a bit earlier you get more engine brake and shift temp from fronts more to the backs
## 2.9 • Cold Tires in iRacing

- The colder the tire the more a spin can reduce grip
- Less grip fronts = understeer
- Less grip rear = oversteer

## 3.9 - The Maximum Rotation Point

- We are more efficient at trail braking than accelerating gradually so MRP happens a bit before the apex.
- Acceleration is more abrupt so car rotates more so it needs to be pointed more before
- You almost always accelerate a bit before the apex
- The more straight you have after the corner the more you benefit from accelerating before the apex
- Low downforce cars lose immediately ability to turn with higher speed vs high downforce cars
- That’s why high df corner shapes are more round while low df more V
- Most important things when learning a corner are braking point and where MRP is
- YAW rate in telemetry is how much car is rotating
## 3.10 - Direction of Traction Forces

- Sum of forces also points towards the exit
## 3.11 - MRP mistakes, problems and solutions

- Relationship between steering and YAW is what tells you if it’s oversteer of understeer
- Do not add more steering when accelerating
  - otherwise you abuse the fronts
## 3.12 - When to Late Apex

- Late apex is still pretty close to the apex
- Late apex benefit depends on how long the straight is after the corner
## 3.13 - Double Apexes

- Corner where you touch the inside, then you go a little wide, then you touch inside again
- You should turn early with too much speed, then you get MRP in the middle of the two apexes
- Also depends on df
  - Low df = more V
  - High df = more U
## 3.14 - Compound Corners

- Corners that are so close that you have not the time to do the ideal line for both 
- Rule #1 is always be on the limit in between them
  - Never go straight in between corners
- You have to choose what corner to sacrifice most
  - Generally you sacrifice the first one more than the second
  - Depends on how long is the straight after the second one 
- If second corner is larger radius, don’t sacrifice first one
  - do double apex on the second

## 4.2 - Driving the Line vs Driving the Car

- You should drive the car (= stay on the limit) e not over fixate on the right line
- As soon as you go a bit wide you need to drive the car and not the line
  - You can’t go back to the apex anymore otherwise you spin
- Objective is drive the line and drive the car at the same time
## 4.3 - Braking Functions

- Two functions
  - Slow down the car
  - Increase rotation
    - Because inertia shifts weight to the front
      - More grip to the front = more rotation
      - Rear tires lift = less rear grip = less resistance to rotation
    - [ ] Relax your hands when braking so you accomodate rotation

## 4.6 - Trail Braking

- What is trail braking
  - braking + cornering (steering)
  - vs coasting = letting a car into a corner with 0% brakes
- Why we do trail brake
  - use all the grip of all tires
  - brake deeper into the corner
- Trail braking only works together with steering
  - otherwise you are under the limit
- How much should I trail brake
  - Enough to keep neutral steer
    - Oversteer → release brakes
    - Understeer → bake more
  - also depends on the car
    - Low df = keep braking for longer
    - high df = less trail braking
- You need to feel the difference of releasing slowly 
- As you release the brakes, always add steering 
  - don’t add steering without brakes
- Also the opposite: a tiny amount of throttle shifts weight back and makes you more understeer
  - maintenance throttle
    - correction technique, not to be used always
## 4.7 - Steering

- More force = steer more quickly
- Light hands technique
  - Forget steering angle
  - Goal is to focus on force
  - Focus on the right amount of force and steering will take care of itself
- Self-centering force = tendency to go back to center
- Counter-steer
  - natural, passive reaction by force feedback, useful to use for self-correction
  - you need light hands for this
  - Passive correction >> active correction
- If corner is flat, grip is already there, you can steer fast
- Linear steering vs exponential steering
  - Low df = exponential steering
  - High df = linear steering

## 4.9 - Camber and Elevation Changes

- On a crest = less grip
  - can’t rotate a lot
- Keep hands relaxed and let force feedback do its own thing
- Camber
  - On Camber = same direction of the corner = more grip
  - Off Camber = opposite the direction of the corner = less grip

## 4.11 - Rotation to Speed vs Speed to Rotation

- Speed → Rotation
  - Increase gradually speed and deal with rotation later
- Rotation → Speed
  - Try to rotate the car more, and increase speed later
  - Focus on testing the grip of the car
