# Using LLMs at Oxide

Values in LLM usage ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdrkr1a97xsczg0gzvsxd0))

Oxide employees bear responsibility for the artifacts we create, whatever automation we might employ to create them ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrds4af01g93qxsapgrj4ff))

Rigor ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrds90p8kdept3dfk0mgk27))

LLMs are useful in as much as they promote and reinforce our rigor. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdskcnxrevhav2r7azvnwj))

Empathy ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdsr20wk8ed1aqr9gnakvs))

As we use LLMs, we must keep in mind our empathy for that human, be they the one who is consuming our writing, or the one who has written what we are reading. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdsz7npn30sbavt51txwsx))

Teamwork ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdt5eke875mceb1t2k9239))

Urgency ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdtj2a7mpnaz38a5yxadrr))

Urgency is certainly important, and LLMs absolutely afford an opportunity to do work more quickly — but that pace must not come at the expense of our responsibility, rigor, empathy and teamwork. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdv582nnmgm1dh0nbt5sv1))

LLMs can be excellent editors. Engaging an LLM late in the creative process (that is, with a document already written and broadly polished), allows for LLMs to provide helpful feedback on structure, phrasing, etc. — all without danger of losing one’s own voice ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdx5wepne0dvwach1a4xrd))

LLM-generated writing undermines the authenticity of not just one’s writing but of the thinking behind it as well ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kcrdy604q1wb0xa96vt93wc1))
