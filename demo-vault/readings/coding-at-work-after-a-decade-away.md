# Coding at Work (After a Decade Away)

Dubious return-on-effort of manager coding ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f5nv32s1bj3j3e0db3nbm))

when I became a manager of managers, I stopped writing software at work. I *wanted* to write software, but it simply felt like a lower return on time than doing something else. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f69cj417sb0h28zy5zqvn))

Ultimately, I got stuck in the trap that it was always clear that writing software would be valuable, but it would always be less valuable than another endeavor. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f6jj0a1ck80ah7gdj7j90))

The same wasn’t true when it came to *understanding* the codebase, and I’ve tried–to various degrees of success–to build a workable degree of awareness of the codebases I’ve managed ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f70f88w1c9ywc80xqdczp))

never been truly successful at doing this ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f75bwqyt593atkb6hq2rm))

You can get very far with a minimal understanding of a codebase and surrounding yourself with strong engineers who you can ask useful questions to and get honest answers. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f7gxgpqy5rdzcha75e2er))

The biggest specific challenge for me when it came to writing software as a manager was finding blocks of thinking time ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0f8jz4ht1qxdv71xkf6j3j))

Most importantly, you can do each of those in a few minutes at a time. Between meetings at work, I generally pop back into one of several Claude Code sessions to see where it got to on a given task, review the code, and suggest next steps. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0ffa57ec0eqy8c97qhna56))

As I’ve gotten more knowledgable at both, I’ve refound my ability to write software at work because I can make progress in the small chunks of time between other projects ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0ffzbrvykxpchf85ttqf3e))

they also make it easier than ever to write unhelpful software at work ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0fgfzcan1b2scbmep7690m))

Hold myself to a higher bar than I would hold others in terms of fully releasing my software, monitoring its release, and solving bugs it creates. If I don’t have time to do those things, then I am stealing time from the team rather than helping them. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0fh68se0ypaagrpst7m94f))
