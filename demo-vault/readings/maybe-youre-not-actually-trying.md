# Maybe You’re Not Actually Trying

Why did it take another person getting involved for me to realize I wasn’t Actually Trying? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0g7w737vg0djhm4ncey42s))

When the stalker entered my life, I was at a low point in personal capacity — broke, alone, addled, etc. My approach towards him at that point (ignore, hoping he’d stop) was the only one that seemed available given my spiritual and psychological resources at the time. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0g88hexgcan9bfm9nwvf28))

my orientation to the problem became fixed in time at that point of low agency, and it never occurred to me to revisit it as my capacity for action increased. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0g8fkw8fn2j4gsbk7f0my0))

People are not just high-agency or low-agency in a global sense, across their entire lives. Instead, people are selectively agentic. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0g8qtthnwwzh3260e7k66w))

Let’s say that life is divided up into three theaters: work, relationships with others (all kinds) and relationship to self (physical health, introspection, emotional development, all of it). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0g9126m89ck3f4qk92qzsd))

I think it’s the rule, rather than the exception, that people are stuck at an earlier stage of development in at least one area. There is one theater of life where they’re not Actually Trying ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0g9e6cetgrpxkn27vnrzar))

There is a relevant concept from Alexander Technique that I love, “faulty sensory appreciation,” which I learned about from [Michael Ashcroft](https://expandingawareness.org/). The concept is that habitual tension distorts your sensory impressions—rigid stillness via bodily tension starts to feel like “good posture,” whereas relaxed uprightness feels strange, to the extent you might feel like your back is at a weird angle when you’re actually standing up tall. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kb0gbchsn9104qp7rznh5zyy))
