# Invert, Always Invert

Software engineering is tough. No matter how hard we try and how hard we plan, we always end up missing simple things ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0ztdf3hdyh4t4xdqv7mf5p))

The question, therefore, is *why*? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0zthvkqvesvweh00zawyv4))

it’s because we apply mental models that are far *too optimistic* to our planning ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0ztq3j6n20hw45qprbvjm5))

can we instead use a *pessimistic* mental model? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0zv1vk105eecy81fdhy5zt))

As Charlie says: “Invert, always invert.” This is the way that you can save yourself from disaster. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0zvcvbhj0jebg4rr0kdhg6))

For example, if your goal is to keep your home clean, instead of thinking about what it means for it to be spotless, you can *invert* the problem by thinking about what it would mean for it to be disgusting, and then make sure you do everything possible to make it *not* disgusting. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0zvrwc47t3md092dz4thnt))

I’d like to propose that the next time you do a significant piece of work, you do an *inversion pas* ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kc0zxnxnpxm6wawvnaa24zzh))
