# My Work-from-home Setup

### 📑 Materials

---

At the end of 2022 I wrote a newsletter edition about [my work-from-home setup](https://refactoring.fm/p/my-desk-setup-and-accessories). 

It was around Christmas time, so I thought it could help with gifts. Surprisingly, it became one of the most popular posts of the year, and I spent the following week replying to readers who asked clarifications about various items.

Fast forward one year, I have been working on a **bigger and better edition** of it, thanks to the help of the community. The thread on this got a ton of responses 👇 and—not gonna lie—it made me impulse-buy a lot of new stuff.

![image](../assets/e76ac2b558bf4a20.png)

So, today I will recommend and review **20+ items** (up from 10 last time). These include both the ones I have been using myself (old and new), and the most popular ones from the community.

I will also organize things into categories:

- 🪑 **Desk & Health** — the standing desk routine and everything around it.

- 🎙️ **Video / Audio** — the setup for recording audio, video, or simply having calls.

- 🖥️ **Tech** — display, docks, chargers, and more.

- ➡️ **Next up** — new, promising items I am considering buying.

- 💬 **From the Community** — recommended pieces from Refactoring readers. From simple ones, like monitor lights, to wild ones, like the XReal glasses.

Also, I am not affiliated to any of these products and I don’t make money from doing this. Just genuine recommendations from myself and the community.

Let’s dive in! 👇

---

### 🪑 Desk + Health

Before we start, here is a full picture of my desk, with some notes about the main items 👇  let’s look at them one by one.

![image](../assets/1606d2abe7b34cdd.jpg)

#### 1) [Fully Jarvis Standing Desk](https://store.hermanmiller.com/brands-fully?lang=en_US)

Last year I recommended using a standing desk, and I still *stand* (hehe) by that. 

I have been using it for about 1.5 years now — as of today I stand the whole morning, and in the afternoon I alternate between sitting and standing. The breakdown is around 60% standing and 40% sitting.

Standing helps me in two main ways:

1. ⬆️ **Posture **— I suffered from bad posture my whole life. This improved a lot over the last year, and I even got friends who told me (unsolicited) that I looked taller.

1. 🔋 **Energy** **&** **Focus **—  I just feel better throughout the day. Whenever I sit for a long time, I notice I tend to become lazy and unfocused, whereas this never happens when I am standing. Now I am doing my best work while standing (like writing this newsletter), which is has been a welcome surprise.

As for the desk itself, I use a Fully Jarvis. Last year Fully has been acquired by Hermann Miller and unfortunately many products and geographies were discountinued. You can still buy the desks, but not in Europe for example.

However, standing desks have become quite popular and you have many alternatives. Here are the most popular ones:

- [**Autonomous**](https://www.autonomous.ai/) — Autonomous has become a household name for office furniture. It makes everything from desks to chairs to accessories, and also acts as reseller for many great other office brands.

- [**Flexispot**](https://www.flexispot.it/) — if you are in Europe, Flexispot has a pretty wild variety of desks and configurations, it is well reviewed, and has great value for money. 

- [**IKEA**](https://www.ikea.com/us/en/p/bekant-desk-sit-stand-white-s49022538/) — yes, IKEA makes standing desks, too. They are more basic compared to other brands, but they are sturdy and work well. I bought one to create a second working space for friends and collaborators, and I can’t complain.

#### 2) [Herman Miller Sayl](https://www.hermanmiller.com/products/seating/office-chairs/sayl-chairs/)

For when I am sitting, I replaced the [Ikea active stool](https://www.ikea.com/us/en/p/lidkullen-sit-stand-support-gunnared-dark-gray-50445787/) I was using last year with a Hermann Miller Sayl.

![image](../assets/25e23662d1394655.png)

First, about the stool: when you research standing desks you hear a lot about the benefits of using a stool: it keeps your spine active, prevents bad posture when you are sitting, etc.

I don’t know — these benefits didn’t really click for me. The stool isn’t comfortable and, when I have been standing for a long time and I want to switch to sitting, I *want *to be comfortable.

So I have looked at many chairs and the Sayl hit a sweet spot for me: it has the benefits of an office chair — adjustable, good support, etc — it is good-looking and not too office-y for my taste, and it is not overly expensive.

#### 3) [Standing Mat](https://www.amazon.com/ComfiLife-Anti-Fatigue-Floor-Mat/dp/B0797Q5HL7/ref=sr_1_18?crid=19380YNSBEP4H&keywords=standing%20mat&qid=1705914323&sprefix=standing%20m%2Caps%2C184&sr=8-18)

If you are using a standing desk you *need* a standing mat, otherwise your feet will get sore in no time — unless you are using shoes, which is not my thing though.

![image](../assets/76ddf34f79cf4780.jpeg)

You can find many mats on Amazon. My advice is not to buy cheap ones: in order to be effective, the mat needs to be *dense*, which makes it more expensive. If you can, go for a $30+ mat,

#### 4) [Oakwood desk shelf](https://oakywood.shop/products/desk-shelf-dual-monitor-stand)

Last year I was using a [**IKEA SIGFINN Stand**](https://www.ikea.com/gb/en/p/sigfinn-monitor-stand-fixed-height-bamboo-veneer-20362929/)** **as desk shelf. Honestly it’s fantastic for its price and I continue to recommend it — but it’s also a bit small and I have been searching for something wider where I could fit more stuff.

![image](../assets/0f966632a7cd4ef4.png)

So a few months ago I went for this [shelf by Oakwood](https://oakywood.shop/products/desk-shelf-dual-monitor-stand) and I have been happy with it ever since. It’s beautiful, well-made, and cheaper than other more established brands.

### 🎙️ Video / audio

I have paid more attention to my video+audio setup in recent months since I decided to start a podcast. Here are my essentials:

#### 5) [Shure MV7](https://www.shure.com/en-US/products/microphones/mv7?variant=MV7-K) ($249)

Same mic I was using last year — it’s great and I have zero complaints. It is probably the last mic you will ever buy.

I am using it through the XLR port, and send it to my Scarlett interface (more below).

#### 6) [Elgato Low-profile Arm](https://www.amazon.com/Elgato-Wave-Mic-Arm-Microphone/dp/B097376LKF) ($80)

I replaced my previous arm with this one and I just love it.

Being low profile it has a longer reach and it stays out of the way when I am on video, plus it has great cable management. Extra points for being white.

![Elgato low-profile arm + Shure MV7](../assets/71ccf68b96984ef0.png)

#### 7) [Focusrite Scarlett 2i2](https://us.focusrite.com/products/scarlett-2i2) ($199)

The MV7 has a USB port so this audio interface is not essential. I use it for two reasons:

- **Quality **— the “Air” mode noticeably improves the sound quality, and it’s also convenient to have the gain knob always available to adjust levels. The MV7 has touch controls but they feel a bit finicky.

- **Recording** — I am an amateur guitar player so I use the same interface to plug guitar and record music from time to time.

![image](../assets/ed483beff04f403a.jpg)

#### 8) [Camo + iPhone as webcam](https://reincubate.com/camo/) (Free or $50/year, plus the iPhone)

I have been using my iPhone as a webcam for more than two years and, even now with the podcast, I am not thinking of changing this anytime soon.

Quality is higher than any webcam on the market. The only way to top it is to go for a proper SLR / mirrorless camera with a good lens, that will give you a better bokeh. But I don’t think it’s essential, even as a full-time creator.

You can do it with the default Continuity Camera feature, but personally I am using Reincubate Camo, because it has more controls and is a bit more reliable.

![iPhone-as-a-webcam mode](../assets/bec6c1423e9e4dc4.png)

#### 9) [Small magnetic stand](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0B492MLPR/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_asin_title_o08_s00?ie=UTF8&psc=1) ($20)

When I use my iPhone as a webcam, I put it on this small stand (see picture above). Nothing fancy: just simple and convenient.

#### [Logitech Litra Glow](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/lighting/litra-glow.946-000001.html) ($60)

I replaced my ring light with the Litra Glow last year, and I am loving it. It is small, attaches well to the monitor, and it gets the job done.

![image](../assets/8d47eb03a5004397.png)

Also, you can tilt it and it basically doubles as a monitor light. I keep it on all day.

### 🖥️ Tech

Let’s now talk about displays, docks, chargers, and more.

#### 10) [Apple Studio Display](https://www.apple.com/studio-display/)

I have been a happy owner of a 27’ LG Ultrafine for several years, until it unexpectedly broke last year and I had to look for new monitor.

I have a Macbook, and the sad reality is that if you want a screen with the same pixel density as your Mac, options are extremely few. Basically you only have the Apple Studio Display or this new [Samsung one](https://www.samsung.com/us/computing/monitors/5k/27-viewfinity-s9-5k-monitor-with-thunderbolt-4-matte-display-and-smart-features-ls27c900panxza/) which was about the same price.

![Apple Studio Display + VESA Arm](../assets/670ea855abcd4095.jpg)

So I made the leap and bought the Studio Display despite the mixed reviews, and honestly, I am very happy with it. The display is gorgeous: it is basically a 27’ Macbook display, the build quality is great, and the native integration means that everything just works: true tone, adjusting brightness from the function keys, etc.

#### 11) [Jarvis Monitor Arm](https://store.hermanmiller.com/office-furniture-desk-accessories-organization/jarvis-single-monitor-arm/2548325.html?lang=en_US) ($100)

I bought the screen with the VESA mount instead of the stand, because I already had a VESA arm. I had bought it from Fully, together with the desk, which means now you can get it from Hermann Miller.

However, it’s quite a tough sell now as, for some reason, the price doubled from $50 to $100, and you can find plenty of capable arms on Amazon for much less.

#### 12) [CalDigit TS3](https://www.caldigit.com/ts3-plus/) ($240)

I resisted buying this — despite so many people recommending it — for a long time because I was unsure about spending that much money on a dock.

Boy was I wrong.

Right now I am using it to connect: the mic (through the audio interface), the display, the Litra Glow, the Stream Deck (more later), the iPhone (as webcam), and a couple of charging cables.

![image](../assets/c6f1899d576546e7.png)

All of this is hidden under the shelf and connected to the Macbook through a single usb-c cable, which is tidy and convenient. Before that, I was plugging/unplugging stuff all the time, and I had cables everywhere on the desk.

#### 13) [Belkin 2-in-1 MagSafe Charger](https://www.belkin.com/2-in-1-wireless-charger-stand-with-official-magsafe-charging-15w-certified-refurbished/P-WIZ010-RM2.html) ($70)

I have an iPhone and Airpods, and I use this stand to keep them in charge while working. The new standby mode on iOS can also display calendar / tasks and is a niche touch.

### 💬 From the community

Finally, here items recommended in the community — I am picking the most popular ones and those that looked the most interesting to me.

#### 17) [Two-tier standing desk • from Flexispot](https://www.flexispot.com/vici-duplex-standing-desk)

Why go for a desk + shelf when you can directly buy a *two-tier* desk? This has to be the question that [Kishan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kishan-sampat/?originalSubdomain=in) asked himself when he went for the [Flexispot Vici](https://www.flexispot.com/vici-duplex-standing-desk).

It’s a cool concept that includes interesting solutions for cable management, and a bulletin board. 

#### 18) [Monitor top light • Yeelight](https://en.yeelight.com/product/led-screen-light-bar-pro/)

Monitor top lights have become increasingly popular in the past few years, often replacing lamps entirely.

Ross bought a [Yeelight](https://en.yeelight.com/product/led-screen-light-bar-pro/), which is well reviewed and compatible with smart home ecosystems.

#### 19) [Logi MX Vertical Mouse](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/mx-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.910-005447.html)

Vertical mouses are popular both in the Refactoring community and among my friends. The [Logi MX](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/mx-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.910-005447.html) takes the crown, but many also use the [Lift](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/lift-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.html).

I have never used them because I am more of a trackpad guy, but people who try them usually don’t go back.

#### 20) [Timeflip time tracker](https://timeflip.io/)

This little dice is suprisingly popular! Both [Nicola](https://hybridhacker.email/) and [Shreyas](https://www.linkedin.com/in/shreyaspatil/) (30+ years of exp combined) recommended it as a simple way to track how you spend your time.

![image](../assets/d186f4663976454c.png)

I bought one myself, so I will let you know!

#### 21) [Opal C1 Webcam](https://opalcamera.com/opal-c1)

As far as regular webcams go, the Opal is the most recommended one in my circles. It is praised for its video quality and its software.

#### 22) [XReal Air Glasses](https://www.xreal.com/air/)

Let’s close with a wild pick! [Brian](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sherbang/?originalSubdomain=nl), CEO of Gozynta, reported on using the XReal glasses when travelling 👇

> *When I travel, I’ve been using a pair of XReal air glasses. This gives me more privacy, and better battery life than using my laptop screen. The compromise is a loss of some image clarity near the edges, and looking like a dork. I spent 2 full days working on a train with this setup on our last trip.*

I expect AR/VR headsets to become increasingly popular for work, especially for people who travel a lot. I have never tried the XReal, but I have a Quest 3 and the virtual monitor is surprisingly viable. 

Granted, an actual monitor is still superior, and I work 95% from home so I can’t say I use the Quest for work, but I could totally see myself using it in a hotel, or a train.
