### **1) 🎙️ Pramoda's journey (**[**02:11**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4KeoO_Ql0k&t=131s)**)**

Pramoda shares his journey from a junior engineer to a leadership and communication coach:

#### **From engineer to coach**

Pramoda started his career as a junior engineer at Oracle in 2002. His biggest challenge was communication, with English being his third language. He explains:

> *"My biggest challenge back then was communication. One of the challenges was the language itself, English being my third language, I struggled to communicate and share my ideas with people."*

#### **Career progression**

Within six years, Pramoda's improved communication skills opened new opportunities:

- Transitioned from software engineer to product manager

- Became an enterprise data architect

- Moved into engineering management roles

He emphasizes the impact of communication on his career:

> *"None of that would happen actually without the communication skills I developed during my career. My career trajectory completely changed. It gave me new opportunities because I was ready. I was ready beyond my engineering skills to communicate, articulate ideas, both in writing and verbal communication."*

#### **The Toastmasters experience**

Pramoda stresses the importance of the local Toastmasters Club for his improvement. Toastmasters focuses on communication, public speaking, and leadership skills. Pramoda describes a typical meeting:

> *"Essentially, each club meeting is about 60 to 90 minutes. And there are three things that happen in each club meetings. One is called prepared speeches, where people come prepared on a topic. They speak for five to seven minutes... The second section is called a table topic section, where you will be given a topic, any random topic... The last section is where there is evaluation."*

### **2) 🗣️ The importance of communication skills (**[**09:56**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4KeoO_Ql0k&t=596s)**)**

Pramoda discusses why engineers often struggle with communication and the importance of good communication skills:

1. **Language barrier** — many engineers are non-native English speakers, making it difficult to articulate ideas effectively.

1. **Curse of knowledge** — Engineers with deep expertise often struggle to explain complex concepts in simple terms.

> *"They have so much knowledge, deep knowledge and expertise about something. They think they're adding value by giving all the details... They think they're helping, but actually they're not helping by complicating the matter."*

Pramoda highlights two key areas where communication skills made a difference in his career:

1. **Personal impact** — improved verbal + written comms led to greater influence in meetings, documents, and negotiations.

1. **Career opportunities** — better communication skills opened doors to diverse roles beyond traditional engineering paths.

> *"We need to be able to explain complex ideas to a fifth grader. If you can explain complex ideas to a fifth grader at age 11, you are a good communicator."*

### **3) 🏗️ The TALL framework (**[**13:19**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4KeoO_Ql0k&t=799s)**)**

Pramoda introduces his TALL framework for effective communication:

#### **(T) Talking**

Focus on being intentional in your talking. Focus on making enough pauses and reducing filler words:

> *"The easiest way to change your behavior is work with your team and say, 'I'm going to give a presentation today, maybe five minutes or 10 minutes. I want you to watch my presentation and count the number of fillers I use.'"*

#### **(A) Asking**

Ask open-ended questions from a place of curiosity rather than judgment:

> *"What's the rationale behind your decision? Or what's the reasoning for making this choice versus that choice? Now, look at the question here. I am believing that you have a reason for that."*

#### **(L) Listening**

Improve listening skills by removing distractions and practicing mindful breathing:

> *"By being more intentional, by pausing, I teach some breath work, so they actually can take slow, deep breaths as they're listening. So they not only listen to words, but they listen to emotions of people."*

#### **(L) Leadership**

Develop leadership communication skills through coaching and practice:

> *"If you ever work with a coach, my strong recommendation is start early. You don't have to wait to become a manager to hire a coach."*

### **4) 👥 Handling emotions in conversations (**[**45:29**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4KeoO_Ql0k&t=2729s)**)**

Pramoda shares strategies for managing emotions in professional conversations:

#### **The 4F framework**

Understand the path to action using the 4F framework: Facts, Fiction, Feelings, and Fierce action. 

> *"In terms of any discussion, especially difficult conversations, people having emotional discussion and perspective differences, it is always the second step that is challenging, which is the fiction or the story I am telling about you or the situation."*

#### **Acknowledging emotions**

In emotionally charged conversations, Pramoda suggests:

> *"By simply saying, wow, I hear a lot of passion behind what you said, you are acknowledging that in this room, there is a strong emotion to be discussed. You're not saying it is good or bad."*

#### **De-escalation techniques**

To de-escalate tense situations, Pramoda recommends the AMPP technique:

> *"AMPP is an acronym. Ask, Mirror, Paraphrase, and Prime. You ask more questions, open-ended questions... You can paraphrase. You can say, 'Let me summarize so far. You said these things.'"*

### **5) 👨‍💼 Leadership development and coaching (**[**50:29**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4KeoO_Ql0k&t=3029s)**)**

Pramoda stresses the importance of leadership development and professional coaching.

> *"Working with a coach is the highest form of changing yourself as a person. And I strongly admire you if you already have a coach. Kudos to you. You have the humility to get help and the courage to change yourself."*

#### **Coaching vs. mentoring**

Pramoda clarifies the difference:

> *"One word for mentors is instructions. They give you instructions on achieving something... Whereas a coach is somebody who is more into inquiry, who will ask you great questions to look at what is the limiting factor in your mind."*

#### **Getting started with coaching**

If coaching is not accessible, Pramoda recommends reading “What Got You Here Won't Get You There*”.*

> *"Pick the book, What Got You Here Won't Get You There. That book by Dr. Marshall Goldsmith has everything you need for leadership development... If you read these two books and apply everything you learned from these two books, your leadership can change within 12 months."*
