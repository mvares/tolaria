# Interview with Guillermo Rauch

### Interview

---

Today's guest is Guillermo Rauch — CEO and founder of Vercel.

With Guillermo, we talked about his journey in tech, from a small-town in Argentina, to successful open-source developer, to CEO of a billion-dollar company. Then we covered the unique Vercel model, which combines open-source and commercial work. And finally, we discussed the future of AI, engineers, open-source and software engineering at large.

### **1) 🌟 From Argentina to Silicon Valley (**[**02:57**](https://youtu.be/5Q4FasJl0SA&t=177s)**)**

Guillermo's journey from a humble neighborhood in Argentina to founding a billion-dollar company is deeply intertwined with his passion for the web and open source tech.

> ***"If it wasn't open source and it wasn't web based, if it wasn't for those two pillars, I would not be where I am. I tried everything else... but there's a longevity and a dopamine hit to being able to create something and share it as a URL."***

Growing up in Lanùs, a small area in the outskirts of Buenos Aires, Guillermo discovered the power of the web at a young age. At just 16, his open-source project called "fancy menu" was featured on a popular blog, giving him his first taste of global recognition.

The democratizing nature of the web - how it allows anyone, regardless of location or privilege, to share their ideas globally - became foundational to his vision for Vercel. This philosophy drives Vercel's mission to enable anyone to create exceptional web experiences, from beginners working on their first project to teams building top-30 internet websites.

### **2) 🧩 Progressive Disclosure of Complexity (**[**10:31**](https://youtu.be/5Q4FasJl0SA&t=631s)**)**

One of Vercel's core design principles is what Guillermo calls "*progressive disclosure of complexity*" - creating technology that's approachable for beginners but powerful enough for enterprise needs.

This principle guides the design of Next.js, which starts with minimal code (as little as one line) but scales to power some of the internet's most sophisticated websites.

> ***"I'm building a platform that should not be intimidating for your first line of code, and gives you the superpower that you could be one day a top 30 internet website with the same infrastructure, with the same tools, with the same access."***

Guillermo draws inspiration from products like the iPhone, which can be used by both children and the elderly while still serving business professionals with complex needs.

His approach to API design focuses on what he calls "token minimization" - requiring minimal code to get started, then gradually introducing more sophisticated features as needed:

- 🏁 **Low barrier to entry** — a new Next.js project can be started with just a few lines of code.

- 🌱 **Growing with users** — complexity is only introduced when needed for specific requirements.

- 🔄 **Same technology at all scales** — the technology that powers a beginner's first project is the same that can scale to handle millions of users.

### **3) 🔄 Balancing Open Source and Commercial Success (**[**21:36**](https://youtu.be/5Q4FasJl0SA&t=1296s)**)**

Vercel has achieved a rare balance between open-source contribution and commercial success. When asked about potential conflicts between these two worlds, Guillermo emphasizes that there's no inherent conflict when approached with the right mindset.

> ***"The best thing that could happen to Next.js is for the pie to get so large that we're just really happy to have a slice. Rather than me saying, 'I have 1.3 million developers, how can I get those 1.3 million developers using Vercel?' That's not how we think."***

The key insights from Vercel's approach include:

- 🌐 **Focus on adoption over capture** — making the open-source project as successful as possible creates a larger ecosystem that benefits everyone.

- 🧰 **Natural complements** — the commercial offering should provide value that wouldn't naturally be expected in the open-source project.

- 🛠️ **Sustainable funding** — the commercial business creates a funding mechanism for the intensive work required to maintain and grow open-source projects.

Guillermo rejects the "OpenCore" model that provides a lesser version of the product for free. Instead, Vercel has worked to make its APIs accessible even to competitors, ensuring the open-source technologies remain valuable regardless of which infrastructure they run on.

For those looking to build successful open-source businesses, he suggests **finding natural complements** like managed infrastructure or AI capabilities that extend the value of the open project without diminishing it.

### **4) 🤖 AI, React, and the Future of Frameworks (**[**30:35**](https://youtu.be/5Q4FasJl0SA&t=1835s)**)**

Guillermo shares a provocative perspective on the future of web frameworks in the age of AI: React may be the last major framework of its kind, as future frameworks will be designed with AI collaboration in mind.

> ***"My colleague who's our VP of AI, Jared Palmer says, it's very likely that React and Vue and Svelte are the last frameworks... AI decides that the tool is better for its outputs rather than like a first principles new framework."***

This shift is already happening with v0, Vercel's AI coding tool, which Guillermo describes as "developer experience 2.0." While traditional frameworks represent human engineers' best thinking on how to make other humans productive, AI will fundamentally change this dynamic.

What makes a framework "AI-friendly" differs from what makes it human-friendly:

- 📚 **Documentation and examples** — extensive training data helps AI understand how to properly use the framework.

- 🧩 **Local reasoning** — components that can be understood in isolation without global side effects.

- 🔄 **Composability** — the ability to work on small slices of a problem that fit together like Lego bricks.

The challenge AI currently faces with large codebases isn't just about intelligence but also about context management - how to process and reason about many files and their relationships simultaneously. Frameworks that facilitate cleaner separation of concerns will likely have advantages in AI collaboration.

### **5) 🚀 Technical Leadership as a CEO (**[**48:26**](https://youtu.be/5Q4FasJl0SA&t=2906s)**)**

Even as CEO of a billion-dollar company, Guillermo maintains his technical edge and passion for the underlying technology. While he doesn't code as part of his day job, he stays deeply engaged with technical details.

His approach to technical leadership centers on having a well-calibrated compass:

> ***"I think a big problem with being the CEO of a technical company is when your compass cannot be accurately calibrated. Every CEO would not dispute that their applications have to be fast. But what is North? How do you calibrate with great precision the needle of that compass?"***

This technical depth provides several advantages:

- 🔍 **Quality feedback** — ability to evaluate work from first principles.

- 💼 **Business conversations** — technical credibility when speaking with prospects and partners.

- 🧠 **Intellectual honesty** — Knowing which battles to pick and when to defer to experts.

Looking forward, Guillermo believes AI will democratize technical and business knowledge, making everyone more "full stack." He shared an example of a deep infrastructure engineer who used v0 to create a professional-grade visualization about traffic patterns during Spain's blackouts — something that would have been impossible for that engineer to do just a few years ago.

> ***"With AI, I think so many more people are going to go through this journey. I think AI will make all of us full stack."***

This democratization of skills will enable more "truth-seeking" — one of Vercel's core values — as both technical and non-technical people gain access to knowledge previously outside their domains.
