# Interview with Francesco Dominidiato

Today's guest is [Francesco Dominidiato](https://it.linkedin.com/in/francescodominidiato)!

Francesco is CTO at [Moneyfarm](https://moneyfarm.com/), one of the largest wealth management companies in Europe, and former CTO at high-growth startups like Casavo and Docebo. During his career he scaled organizations from zero to hundreds of employees, and has incredible experience at all levels: from leading small teams to NASDAQ-listed companies.

With Francesco, we talked about career frameworks, how to create a path that creates opportunities for both individual contributors and managers, how to address career development issues, organizational debt and more.

### 1) 🎯 Careers Frameworks Evolution ([02:54](https://youtu.be/cRhB265AdVs&t=174s))

Francesco shares his perspective on the evolution of engineering career frameworks. The industry went through distinct phases:

- **🛠️ Craftsmanship Era** — early days with minimal structure

- **⚙️ Engineering Approach** — more structured systems emerged

- **🏭 Industrial Phase** — what Francesco calls the "dark period" where companies tried to apply traditional industrial management to software

> *"Linda Hill says that leading innovation is about creating the space where people are willing and able to do the hard work of innovative problem solving"*

The industrial approach created several problems by forcing engineers into management roles to grow, leading to loss of technical expertise in teams, misaligned incentives for career growth, and reduced effectiveness in both technical and management roles.

---

### 2) 🏗️ Building Effective Career Ladders ([09:24](https://youtu.be/cRhB265AdVs&t=564s))

Francesco emphasizes that career frameworks should be treated like software architecture – start simple but plan for scale.

Here are his key principles for building career ladders:

- **🎯 Right-sized Structure** — match the framework complexity to company size

- **🔄 Future-Proof Design** — plan for growth while keeping current simplicity

- **📝 Clear Documentation** — maintain basic role descriptions and expectations

> *"If you are a very small early-stage startup, should you develop with a complicated microservice architecture based on Kubernetes? Absolutely not... The same applies to organizational design"*

The framework should evolve naturally with the organization's growth, similar to how a monolith might eventually split into microservices.

---

### 3) 💰 Salary Ranges & Organizational Debt ([30:31](https://youtu.be/cRhB265AdVs&t=1831s))

One of the most critical aspects of career frameworks is handling compensation properly from the start.

Francesco identifies common pitfalls:

- **📊 Inconsistent ranges** — basing new hires' salaries on their previous compensation

- **💼 Hidden ranges** — lack of transparency in salary bands

- **🏷️ Misaligned levels** — compensation not matching actual role expectations

> *"As proud geek engineers, we love coherency and consistency... If you are coherent and consistent when you write code, then you should be coherent and consistent also when you observe company rules"*

### 4) 📈 Framework Evolution & Assessment ([41:15](https://youtu.be/cRhB265AdVs&t=2475s))

Francesco shares lessons learned from evolving career frameworks at scale:

- **📋 Simplify assessment** — avoid over-complicated evaluation matrices

- **🎯 Focus on impact** — evaluate based on business outcomes, not just technical skills

- **🔄 Regular updates** — frameworks should evolve with company growth

> *"Agility is all about feedback... if for the process assessment of all your population you need to invest one month, maybe this is a bad smell"*

### 5) 👥 Engineering Leadership Roles ([56:32](https://youtu.be/cRhB265AdVs&t=3392s))

Our chat ended with insights on hybrid roles like Tech Leads and Engineering Managers. Here are the key considerations:

- **🔀 Career paths** — visualize as a "Y" shape with clear progression options

- **🎭 Role flexibility** — allow for movement between IC and management tracks

- **⚖️ Balance** — maintain awareness of power dynamics in hybrid roles

> *"Pay attention that people are free to change from one path to the other, even later in their career, but they cannot jump from one branch of the Y to the other branch... You have to go back to the cross"*
