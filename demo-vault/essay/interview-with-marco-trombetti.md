# Interview with Marco Trombetti

Today's guest is Marco Trombetti!

Marco is the CEO and founder of Translated, one of the largest translation companies in the world, powering translations for the likes of Airbnb, Uber, Skyscanner, and more. Translated is also an AI pioneer. It has developed its own models for more than 20 years and has recently released the most advanced translation LLM in the world.

With Marco, we talked about the future of AI, combining AI and human work and the role of regulation. This is also the first interview we ever did in person at the Translated HQ. But as you will see and hear, I was recovering from a bad cough and had a bit of a low voice, hope you don't mind!

### **1) 🚀 From Translation to AI Pioneer (**[**03:52**](https://youtu.be/HgKAg1rtQoE&t=232s)**)**

Marco Trombetti is the CEO and co-founder of Translated, one of the largest professional translation companies globally, serving clients like Airbnb, Uber, and Skyscanner. Founded in 1999, Translated was among the first internet-based translation services.

> *"We started working on something called machine-human symbiosis, so interaction between human and machine. How can we use AI to try to help professional translators be faster?"*

The company's journey reflects the evolution of AI in business:

- 🌐 **Internet Pioneer** — Started as one of the first online translation services

- 🤝 **Human-First Approach** — Initially focused on human translation when AI wasn't reliable

- 🔄 **Continuous Innovation** — Early adoption of machine learning and neural networks for translation

### **2) 🤖 Evolution of Machine Translation (**[**05:59**](https://youtu.be/HgKAg1rtQoE&t=359s)**)**

The translation industry has been at the forefront of AI development, experiencing all major technological shifts:

> *"In 2004, the first machine learning approach started... it was the first time you use data to train the model to learn the rules rather than typing the rules one after the other."*

The progression of translation technology shows how rapidly AI has evolved:

- 📊 **Rule-based Systems** — Early attempts at machine translation through programmed rules

- 🧠 **Statistical Learning** — Shift to data-driven approaches in 2004

- ⚡ **Neural Networks** — Implementation of deep learning, starting with basic models

- 🔄 **Transformer Architecture** — Revolutionary advancement enabling better context understanding

### **3) 🔬 Lara: Next-Gen Translation Model (**[**12:39**](https://youtu.be/HgKAg1rtQoE&t=759s)**)**

Translated recently released Lara, their most advanced AI translation model, which represents a significant leap in translation technology. The model combines traditional translation accuracy with LLM capabilities.

> *"We combine a language model with a typical translation model... translation standard translation was about accuracy, and it was very literal. And on the other side, you have LLMs that understand context, can do instruct, can do reasoning."*

Key innovations include:

- 🎯 **Dual Architecture** — Combines traditional translation accuracy with LLM reasoning

- 💬 **Interactive Capabilities** — Can ask for clarification on ambiguous terms

- ⚖️ **Bias Prevention** — Actively works to prevent gender and cultural biases

### **4) 🤝 Human-AI Symbiosis (**[**32:45**](https://youtu.be/HgKAg1rtQoE&t=1965s)**)**

Translated's approach to AI integration shows how humans and machines can work together effectively:

> *"The distribution of wealth changed completely... people that use AI started getting $100,000, and the people that didn't, they were too slow, too low quality, and they went to zero."*

The impact on the translation industry has been significant:

- 📈 **Market Growth** — The human translation business grew 7% year over year

- 💰 **Wealth Distribution** — AI adoption created a divide between high and low earners

- 🌐 **Increased Demand** — AI enables more content to be translated, expanding the market

### **5) ⚠️ AI Risks & Regulation (**[**45:22**](https://youtu.be/HgKAg1rtQoE&t=2722s)**)**

Marco identifies several concerning trends in AI development that require attention:

> *"No machine should be able to initiate conversations with humans by themselves. This is for spam because we're ready with attention... but also because this is the way you create the most dangerous data ever."*

Key concerns include:

- 🎯 **Personal Alignment** — AI systems could be trained to tell people exactly what they want to hear.

- 🤖 **Proactive AI** — The risk of AI systems initiating contact with humans.

- 📋 **Government Control** — The need for regulation of government AI use, particularly in surveillance.

### **6) 💡 Building AI Companies (**[**56:19**](https://youtu.be/HgKAg1rtQoE&t=3379s)**)**

Marco shares valuable advice for entrepreneurs building AI companies:

> *"Don't put the word AI in your pitch... what I think is truly powerful is when you solve the problem using AI. Something that was slow, expensive in the past, you're magically making it a better product."*

His recommendations for success:

- 🎯 **Focus on Solutions** — Emphasize the problem you're solving, not the AI technology

- 🔄 **Vertical Integration** — Specialize in specific industries where you have domain expertise

- ⚡ **Speed to Market** — Move quickly to establish market presence before general AI catches up
