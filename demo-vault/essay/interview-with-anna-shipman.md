# Interview with Anna Shipman

Today's guest is [Anna Shipman](https://www.annashipman.co.uk/)! Anna is the CTO at Kooth, former Technical Director at the Financial Times, and an accomplished author. 

With Anna we talked about her journey in tech and how to develop good technical strategy, drawing from stories and examples from her 20 plus years of engineering experience.

### 1) 🗺️ Anna’s Journey ([01:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=81s))

Anna's path to technology wasn't straightforward.

She studied philosophy and worked in children's book publishing before discovering her interest in tech. 

She taught herself JavaScript and CSS, and she eventually decided to pursue a more formal engineering education. As she embarked on a career as a software engineer, she moved through various roles across the whole stack, touching frontend, backend, and infrastructure.

#### Government Digital Service (GDS) ([02:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=178s)) 🌍

Anna joined the Government Digital Service (GDS) during a pivotal time when the UK government worked on modernizing its tech infrastructure.

They had a clear mission to bring tech development in-house, with the goal of:

1. Creating strong strategic know-how, and 

1. Saving costs on external contracts.

One of the highlights of that experience was working on the launch of the [gov.uk](http://gov.uk/) website. Anna joined the project when it was in private beta, and contributed to its public launch. 

The project aimed to provide a better user experience for citizens, while also reducing internal costs. 

Because if its **strong mission**, GDS was able to attract top talent who were also often willing to take pay cuts.

#### Transition to Financial Times ([05:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=319s)) 💼

Anna later moved to the Financial Times (FT) as a technical director, drawn by the purpose of supporting free press, and by the opportunity to work with a leaner team that was more focused on delivery.

The role involved managing a team and delivering critical projects like the [FT.com](http://ft.com/) website and app, leveraging her leadership experience from GDS.

#### Role at Kooth ([09:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=544s)) 🧑‍⚕️

In her current CTO role at Kooth, Anna oversees digital mental health services for children and young people. 

Kooth offers self-help content, moderated community forums, and counseling through text-based chats. 

Like with FT, and GDS before that, the choice of joining Kooth was significantly influenced by their strong, purpose-driven mission.

### 2) 🎯 What is Technical Strategy ([12:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=759s))

Anna's roles and work have consistently focused on technical strategy. She has always worked on transformative initiatives for the respective organizations, often with a multi-year outlook.

For Anna, technical strategy is about **aligning tech efforts with business needs**.

A good technical strategy *supports* the business strategy, driving the company’s goals through effective use of technology. 

This alignment ensures that technical work directly contributes to the company’s success.

Anna shared several examples, especially from her tenure at FT and Kooth:

#### **FT — No “next next”**

After the launch of the new website, which was internally called “*next*” and had required a long and intense investment, she shifted her focus to sustainability, to avoid another massive rebuild in the future. 

This led to the “*no next next*” strategy, which emphasized continuous improvement and technical debt prevention.

#### **Kooth — Play to win**

The business strategy here follows the **Play to Win** framework, which separates between areas where you just want to keep a standard (”play to play”), from those where you can truly excel because of your unique value proposition (”play to win”).

Kooth strategy focuses on being extremely intentional about their *play to win* areas, and doubling  down on them.

### 3) 🔄 Evolving Strategy ([23:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=1439s))

A common mistake about strategy is to think at it as a static, fixed artefact. Instead, a crucial part of it is regular evaluation and adaptation.

Anna emphasized that it’s better to **start with an imperfect strategy** and refine it over time through continuous feedback and review, rather than over fixating over getting everything right from the start. Just like software.

One common pitfall in strategy development is treating it as a one-time activity. Anna stressed the importance of ongoing evaluation and communication. Another challenge is ensuring that the strategy is actionable and aligned with the company’s goals. Avoiding these pitfalls requires a proactive approach to strategy management.

### 4) 📢 Strategy and Communication ([24:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=1483s))

Anna recalls that all the successful strategy stories she was part in had one thing in common: **great communication**.

Strategy needs to be communicated *repeatedly* across the organization to ensure alignment. There isn’t such a thing like over-communicating. Clear and possibly redundant communication helps maintain focus and direction.

Also, such communication is not top-down only — it goes both ways. Anna advocates for involving team members at all levels in the strategy development process. 

An inclusive approach brings two strong benefits:

- 🏆 **Quality** — it ensures the strategy benefits from diverse perspectives, especially from the people who are the closest to ground work, and often know better than leaders.

- 💪 **Commitment** — it fosters a sense of ownership among the team. People feel more *committed *when they feel a *co-creation* responsibility towards the plan.

### 5) 🛠️ Creating strategy, in practice ([39:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t_IUAEIuLXY&t=2375s))

Towards the end of our chat, I asked Anna for practical steps to start building a technical strategy from scratch. Here is here framework:

1. 🤏 **Start small** — Begin with a clear understanding of the current situation (what she calls the “diagnosis”).

1. 🍻 **Involve others **— Work with a peer or a small group to develop the strategy collaboratively.

1. 💬 **Communicate** — Ensure that the strategy is communicated effectively across the organization.

1. 🔄 **Get feedback** — Regularly evaluate and adjust the strategy based on feedback.
