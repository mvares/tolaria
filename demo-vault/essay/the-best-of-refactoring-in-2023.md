### 🤗 600 people in the community

This is also the year where the community grew the most! We got from ~200 to 600 members, and we are doing more things together than ever:

- 🎟️ **Events **— we organize live interviews with awesome guests where members can join live and ask questions.

- 📗 **Book Club **— every two months we pick a book, read it, and review it together in a live call. The full write up is then published on the newsletter.

- 🥁 **Show & Tell **— every month we celebrate achievements and small wins of members.

- ➡️ **Coming up **— I anticipate the articles I will write so that people can contribute with ideas and insights. The best ones are included in the newsletter and the author is quoted.

Now, while 600 people is a great number, it is still 600 out of 60,000 — that is, the 1%. 

As much as I promote it and tell everyone that it’s awesome, community is demanding. Community is for people who 1) have the courage to ask for help when they have a challenge, and, in turn, 2) are willing to help others without asking for anything in return.

![Screenshot of a recent book review all together](../assets/fa5a7520a1664685.webp)

These take a bit of time and dedication, and we all go through periods of life where we can’t find room for more things. 

If you do, however, I can assure you that it is worth it. I would love to have you onboard 🚢

### 📖 Guides

This year I started writing [monthly guides](https://refactoring.fm/guides), that have instantly become a huge success!

Each guide puts together **multiple articles** I have written in the past, in a coherent package that you can use and apply at work. They are by far** **the most researched and in-depth pieces I have ever written, and are usually more than twice as long as a regular Refactoring piece.

They are also featured prominently in the Library. Whenever a Guide is available for a topic, it is displayed in full width and recommended as the first piece you should read.

![image](../assets/735d080c819442a7.jpg)

Guides fit the general Refactoring structure, that organizes articles and ideas into tracks and topics. Refactoring covers three main tracks: **Software Engineering**, **Engineering Management**, and **Personal Growth**.

![image](../assets/b46d973517404a66.webp)

Each track is further organized into topics, and for each topic you have several articles. Finally, ideas from articles are also published individually in the Monday emails.

You can navigate the full tree inside the Refactoring Library:

![The Software Engineering track in the Refactoring Library](../assets/08f7ba42328f425a.png)
